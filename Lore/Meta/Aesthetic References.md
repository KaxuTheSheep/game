# Aesthetic References

*The creative works that define the look, feel, and tone of New Halcyon.*

---

## Primary References

| Work | Medium | What It Contributes |
|---|---|---|
| **Cyberpunk 2077** | Game | Scale, conglomerate-neon texture, megabuilding verticality, corporate branding saturation, gang/faction street texture |
| **Blade Runner / Blade Runner 2049** | Film | Rain-soaked noir atmosphere, oppressive architectural scale, class stratification as spatial condition, the weight of a city that doesn't care |
| **Ghost in the Shell** | Anime/Film | AI-labour and body-modification as normalised, institutional ambiguity, clean corporate surfaces over systemic rot, identity and personhood questions |
| **Akira** | Anime/Film | Urban density as barely-contained pressure, institutional violence, the city as a force unto itself, youth and counter-culture in impossible conditions |
| **Elysium** | Film | Class stratification made physically and spatially legible — where you live is literally what you are |
| **Marathon** (Bungie) | Game | Brutalist megastructure aesthetic, retro-futurist runner/courier culture, high-contrast primary-colour gear against grime, corporate-military hardware repurposed by civilians |

---

## Tonal Notes

- The city should feel **lived-in and heavy**, not glossy — the neon is there but it is old and some of it is broken
- **Inequality is spatial** — quality of life is readable from the street, from the buildings, from the road condition underfoot
- **Nobody is the villain** — the system is the villain; individuals within it are mostly doing what makes sense given their position
- **Mundane dystopia** — the horror is in the ordinary, not the exceptional; people have adapted to conditions that should be unacceptable because they have never known anything else

## Secondary / Tonal References (Optional)

- *Snow Crash* — Neal Stephenson novel; corporate franchise city-states, privatised everything, dark humour; closest literary parallel to the worldbuilding model
- *Neuromancer* — Gibson's foundational cyberpunk; texture, not plot
- *Dredd* (2012 film) — megablock as total environment; vertical class stratification
