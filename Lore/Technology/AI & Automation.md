# AI & Automation

*The labour infrastructure of New Halcyon. Mostly imported. Mostly from [[Country C]].*

---

## Overview

By the 2100s, AI and automated systems handle a significant proportion of labour functions in New Halcyon — logistics, manufacturing, data processing, low-tier security surveillance, service work. This is not unique to New Halcyon; it is the global trajectory accelerated by the same economic pressures that produced [[The Catalyst (2039)]]. Automation-driven unemployment was one of the erosion factors of the 2026–2038 period.

What is specific to New Halcyon is the ownership structure: automated labour systems are conglomerate assets, not public infrastructure. The productivity gains from automation flow upward to shareholders, not outward to displaced workers. There is no state to redistribute them. This is not a malfunction of the system — it is the system working as designed.

---

## Country C's Role

The majority of New Halcyon's AI and robotics infrastructure traces back to [[Country C]] — hardware, software, and operational systems developed during Country C's forced-automation era and now licensed commercially worldwide. Country C sells to whoever pays, which in New Halcyon means the conglomerates.

The cultural aesthetic of AI labour in New Halcyon — Ghost in the Shell-influenced, android and synthetic presence as normalised rather than exceptional — reflects this Country C technological inheritance.
