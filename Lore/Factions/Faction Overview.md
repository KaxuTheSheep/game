# Faction Overview

*How power and protection are organised in New Halcyon in the absence of any state.*

---

## The Core Logic

No government means no police, no courts, no public services. Every function a state would perform is either performed privately by someone with a profit motive, performed communally by people with a survival motive, or not performed at all. In New Halcyon all three outcomes coexist simultaneously depending on where you are and who you can pay.

---

## Three-Tier Structure

### Tier 1 — Conglomerate Forces

Private armies belonging to each major conglomerate. Protect directly-owned property: headquarters, premium residential, executive zones, commercial and business districts. Highest quality, most professional, run with PR considerations — these forces are a brand asset as much as a security function.

Commercial and business district security is funded directly by the owning conglomerate. No third-party billing. No competing firms.

Each conglomerate's army is the parent organisation; the conglomerate's security force (protecting civilian-facing property) is a subdivision of that army — not a separate entity.

The [[Continental Freight Authority]] operates at this tier as a non-aligned entity — same capability level, answers to nobody in New Halcyon, origin in [[Country B]].

A past inter-conglomerate conflict — [[The War]] — is the foundational scar tissue of present-day tier-1 relationships. All current turf arrangements, alliances, and grudges trace back to it.

### Tier 2 — Contracted Protection Firms

The de facto residential police of New Halcyon. Apartment blocks pay a protection line-item baked into rent to one of several competing firms. Coverage quality scales directly with what the landlord pays. Blocks can switch providers.

The visible result: different uniforms, colours, and drone liveries mark whose contract covers each block. Your safety is a subscription tier. Walking one block can mean walking from one firm's jurisdiction into another's — or into nobody's.

Commercial and business districts do not use this tier. They are covered directly by Tier 1.

### Tier 3 — Street Level (Militias & Gangs)

Cover residential areas with no Tier 2 contract — the forgotten districts. Two distinct types:

**Militias (3):** more consistent, community-rooted or ideologically-driven. See [[Ninth Watch]], [[Greyback Company]], [[Commonweal]].

**Gangs (multiple):** control their own turf, extract protection fees from residents inconsistently and often predatorially. Named after streets, defunct pre-collapse businesses, or squatted old corporate branding within their territory. See [[Gangs Convention & Index]].

---

## Design Note — Army / Security Subdivision Relationship

Conglomerate armies are the parent. Security forces (civilian-facing protection) are a subdivision of those armies — not peers. When conglomerate security forces appear in Tier 1 context, they are acting as part of the army's operational scope, not as a separate institution.
