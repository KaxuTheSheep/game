# Outskirts Community

*Not a faction. A fact. People living where the city stopped caring.*

---

## What This Is Not

The outskirts community is not an organisation. It has no leadership structure, no founding ideology, no membership. It is not a militia in the sense that [[Ninth Watch]], [[Greyback Company]], or [[Commonweal]] are militias. It is not a gang in the sense that the territory-controlling groups documented in [[Gangs Convention & Index]] are gangs.

It is the name given here to the population of [[The Ruins Pockets]] — people living in pre-Catalyst construction at the outer margins of New Halcyon, outside conglomerate control, outside any formal tier of the city's protection structure.

---

## Who Lives Here

The population of the ruins pockets is not unified by anything except geography and the conditions that geography imposes.

Individual motivations vary significantly. Some residents are here because they cannot afford anywhere inside the city proper. Some are avoiding something — debt, faction entanglements, surveillance, relationships with conglomerate systems they do not want to maintain. Some are operating outside the formal economy in ways that require distance from conglomerate oversight. Some have simply been here long enough that the outskirts have become their home rather than their refuge.

The closest real-world parallel is Mumbai's informal settlements — dense, specific, shaped by individual decisions accumulating over time into something functional without being designed. People know their neighbours out of proximity rather than solidarity. Community exists because it is produced by living together, not because anyone decided to produce it.

---

## How It Functions

Infrastructure is improvised. Buildings are modified according to immediate need with available materials. Markets emerge in spaces that accommodate them. Services appear where there is demand and someone capable of providing them.

The economy runs on what works at this remove from the city's formal systems. [[Currency & Alternatives|Reserve currency]] is used but less trusted than closer to the centre. Closed-community exchange and direct barter are significant. Goods that enter the outskirts through salvage, informal trade, or direct production from the ruins pockets' own residents move through networks of individual relationships rather than anything resembling a formal market.

There is no unified security structure. Residents manage their own safety through the usual combination of local knowledge, community relationships, and the informal social contracts that develop in any dense living situation. This is supplemented increasingly by the presence of outside factions.

---

## Faction Influence

The outskirts community is not outside the city's faction structure. It is at its margin — which means factions with an interest in marginal populations are present and active here.

[[Commonweal]] has extended into the ruins pockets, consistent with their mutual aid orientation. Their presence is welcome in most of the community because they offer resources and structure without demanding ideological commitment or territorial control.

Other non-conglomerate factions operate here. Some are documented elsewhere. Others have their origin in the outskirts themselves and have not yet developed enough presence to appear in the city's wider faction landscape.

The influence of these factions is real and growing. Individual residents — each with their own motivations and relationships — navigate this influence differently. Some welcome it. Some are wary. Some use it instrumentally without caring about its origin.
