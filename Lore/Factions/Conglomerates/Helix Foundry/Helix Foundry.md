# Helix Foundry

*The only faction in New Halcyon trying to solve a problem rather than exploit one. That is precisely what makes them vulnerable.*

---

## Overview

Helix Foundry is a pharmaceutical and biological technology company developing scalable micronutrient supplementation for New Halcyon's population — pills that would address the deficiency conditions produced by long-term exclusive synthetic food consumption. They are not the largest company. They are not the most powerful. They are the only significant faction in the city whose primary motivation is not expansion, control, or profit maximisation.

That distinction does not protect them. In some ways it makes their position harder. Every other major player in New Halcyon operates within a logic that Helix can predict and model. Helix operates outside that logic, which makes them difficult to fully trust and difficult to fully classify.

---

## What They Are Trying To Do

The micronutrient deficiency produced by exclusive synthetic food consumption is a known, documented, and ignored public health crisis. Clarent profits from the deficiency driving demand for premium GMO imports. The CFA profits from those imports moving through their infrastructure. The United Territories profits from being the source. Nobody with the power to solve the problem has an interest in solving it.

Helix Foundry was built around the premise that the problem could be solved independently of the existing power structure — that a scalable, affordable micronutrient pill produced through biological manufacturing could reach the populations that the current system leaves deliberately deficient.

The science works. The production process functions. Scaling it to genuinely population-level availability is the problem they are still solving, and the problem that makes them a threat to enough powerful parties that their survival is not guaranteed.

---

## The Pill Manufacturing Process

Helix's micronutrient pills are produced primarily through insect larvae cultivation and processed animal byproducts — specifically targeting the compound classes that synthetic food manufacturing cannot replicate economically. The production process is scalable in ways that GMO agricultural imports are not. You can grow larvae in a controlled facility in a forgotten district. You cannot grow UT farmland in New Halcyon.

The production method is not publicly advertised. It is also not hidden — the information exists in Helix's operational documentation and would be accessible to anyone with sufficient access and motivation to look. Clarent's intelligence operations are aware of it.

The strategic question of whether this information damages Helix depends entirely on who the target audience is. The population currently eating the cheapest Clarent synthetic lines would not care. They are worried about calories, not ingredient lists. The middle tier residents who are Helix's actual customer base for the pills are more susceptible to the disgust response that insect-derived products can produce in people who have the luxury of being selective. Clarent's communications division holds this information and has not yet deployed it — the calculation is that it is worth more as held leverage than as a deployed weapon while Helix remains small enough to be handled other ways.

## Animal Framework Programme

Helix's biological manufacturing requires consistent compound profiles across production batches. Achieving that consistency with living biological systems — insects, larvae, other organisms — requires precise control over the organisms' metabolic processes. This is where the animal augmentation work began.

Helix developed proprietary biological frameworks to interface with their production organisms — hardware integration layers that allow monitoring and regulation of biological processes with a precision that observation alone cannot achieve. The work began with insects and expanded as the research produced techniques applicable to larger animals.

## Position in the Power Structure

Helix sits in a position that every other major faction finds uncomfortable in different ways:

**Clarent** views them as a slow-building threat to their synthetic food ecosystem and is running a coordinated suppression campaign — poaching key personnel, applying supply chain pressure, and preparing the insect-source information for deployment when Helix becomes threatening enough to justify spending the ammunition.

**The CFA** has no interest in Helix succeeding. Population-scale micronutrient pills would reduce Federal States dependency on UT food imports, which would reduce the volume of goods moving through CFA infrastructure. The CFA has not moved against Helix directly — they are too small to justify the reputational cost of visible action — but they are not neutral.

**Vantage** has a genuine potential interest in Helix. Healthier residents are more productive, more stable, and more attractive as tenants. Helix's pills available as a building amenity in Vantage's premium residential portfolio would be a differentiator. The relationship has not developed yet — Vantage watches and calculates.

**Ardent** is Helix's current protector. The relationship is commercial and transactional rather than ideological — Ardent wants Helix's animal framework technology. See [[Helix - Ardent Relationship]].

---

## The Fundamental Problem

The closer Helix gets to genuine population-scale production the more existentially threatening they become to multiple factions simultaneously. Their success accelerates the forces working against their survival. The city is structurally set up to prevent exactly what they are trying to do — not through coordinated conspiracy but through the separate interests of multiple powerful parties happening to align against them.

Helix does not have a clean long-term plan. They are solving a genuine problem, surviving in the short term through Ardent's protection, and operating in an environment where their eventual fate may be determined by forces they cannot fully control. Whether they achieve their stated mission before those forces resolve against them is an open question.

That absence of a clear long-term plan is not a failure of strategic thinking. It is an honest assessment of their position. A company with their resources and their enemies does not get to plan decades ahead. They get to solve today's problem and try to still exist tomorrow.
