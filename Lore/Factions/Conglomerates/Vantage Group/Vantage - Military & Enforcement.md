# Vantage Group — Military & Enforcement

*You are already inside their territory. They do not need to come to you.*

---

## Overview

Vantage's military and enforcement capability is structurally different from every other Tier 1 force in New Halcyon. Clarent's enforcement arm goes places. Ardent's force projects capability. The CFA secures infrastructure. Vantage does not need to go anywhere because the people they might need to act against are already inside their buildings.

This shapes everything about how their force is organised, deployed, and experienced.

---

## The Building As The Weapon

Vantage's most significant enforcement capability is not their personnel. It is their building management infrastructure. A Vantage building can make a resident's life extremely difficult without any human enforcement officer being involved:

- Access credentials failing at inconvenient times
- Climate systems malfunctioning in specific units
- Commissary access restricted through building management decisions
- Maintenance requests deprioritised indefinitely
- Lift access limited to certain floors

None of this is attributable to a specific decision by a specific person. It is building management. These things happen. The pattern of them happening to specific residents at specific times is only visible if you are looking for it and have access to the building's operational logs, which Vantage controls.

Human enforcement is deployed when the building itself is insufficient — which is rarer than it might seem.

---

## Force Structure

**Building Security** — the visible layer. Personnel present in Vantage managed buildings, handling access control, responding to incidents, maintaining physical presence. These are not soldiers. They are security staff whose primary function is deterrence and incident response rather than combat capability. Professional, uniformed, and deliberately non-threatening in presentation. Vantage's brand is residential comfort. Visible aggression undermines that.

**Enforcement Division** — the less visible layer. Smaller units that handle situations requiring more direct intervention — residents or commercial tenants who have become problems, competitive intelligence on Clarent's commissary operations, physical security for Vantage's data infrastructure. These operate without Vantage branding where possible.

**Military Reserve** — Vantage maintains a genuine military capability consistent with Tier 1 status, but it is the smallest of the major conglomerates' forces relative to their overall size. They have never needed a large military because their leverage is structural rather than physical. The reserve exists to deter rather than to deploy.

---

## Ardent Hardware

Vantage's security and enforcement personnel run Ardent augments and weapons. This is a deliberate commercial relationship — Vantage buys Ardent hardware at preferential rates, Ardent gets revenue and a foothold in Vantage's operational infrastructure without getting structural access to the buildings themselves.

Vantage uses Ardent hardware and employs their own operators. Ardent gets the contract but not the access. Both parties understand this arrangement and neither is fully satisfied with it. Ardent wants deeper integration. Vantage wants to maintain control of their own security infrastructure without depending on a company that would benefit from their weakening.

---

## Intelligence Operations

Vantage's most significant enforcement asset is information. The data flowing through their building systems produces intelligence on residents, commercial tenants, and visiting parties that no conventional surveillance operation could match. People behave differently inside their own homes than anywhere else. Vantage's buildings are full of people behaving as if they are unobserved.

This intelligence capability is primarily used commercially — understanding resident behaviour to optimise building services, identifying commercial opportunities, informing the cold war with Clarent. It has obvious applications for more direct leverage and almost certainly serves those applications in specific cases.

The enforcement division's operations are informed by building data in ways that make their interventions appear remarkably well-timed. They know where people are, when they are likely to be alone, what their daily patterns look like, and which pressure points are most likely to produce the desired response. Human enforcement informed by comprehensive data is more efficient than numbers alone.
