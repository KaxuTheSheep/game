# Vantage Group — Residential Portfolio

*Every person in New Halcyon lives somewhere. A significant portion of them live in Vantage.*

---

## The Vertical City

New Halcyon has no houses. The combination of constant population influx through CFA infrastructure, no urban planning authority to enforce low density zoning, and conglomerate incentives to maximise revenue per square metre means everyone lives vertically. The War accelerated this — significant building stock was destroyed and rebuilt as towers because vertical construction is cheaper per unit than horizontal replacement.

The ultra elite occupy the highest floors of the tallest towers or the rare actual houses inside fortified conglomerate compounds. Everyone else is in a segmented apartment of varying quality. Vantage's portfolio spans most of that spectrum.

---

## Portfolio Tiers

**Premium Residential**
Upper floors of Vantage managed towers in conglomerate adjacent and wealthy districts. Large segmented apartments by New Halcyon standards — which means enough space to have distinct sleeping and living areas rather than a single room. Full building technology integration including photovoltaic coating, self-healing concrete, and building management systems with full sensor coverage. Consistent Tier 2 security coverage. Access to commissaries stocking both synthetic and premium GMO food lines.

Residents here are corporate employees, successful independent professionals, and anyone wealthy enough to choose their building. Vantage charges accordingly.

**Standard Residential**
The core of Vantage's portfolio and the housing type for the majority of New Halcyon's stable working population. Functional and cramped. A standard unit is one room segmented into sleeping and living space with a shared bathroom per floor or per cluster of units depending on building age and renovation history. Building technology is present but not comprehensive — self-healing concrete standard, photovoltaic coating on newer buildings, building management systems handling access control and basic climate without the full sensor integration of premium floors.

Services are reliable because the contracts are maintained. This is the primary value proposition Vantage offers at this tier — not comfort, predictability. The lift works. The heating functions. The security response comes when called.

**Marginal Residential**
Older buildings at the edge of Vantage's managed territory, or buildings inherited from the post-War Ardent absorption that have not been fully renovated. Subdivided spaces where larger original units have been carved into smaller ones by whoever managed the building previously. Building technology is inconsistent — some self-healing concrete, some original construction that predates it, photovoltaic coating absent or degraded.

Tier 2 security coverage exists on paper. Response times are slow. Maintenance requests are deprioritised. Residents here are aware they are at the edge of Vantage's attention and adjust their behaviour accordingly — they do not make complaints that will not be answered, they build informal community structures to compensate for inconsistent services, they know which building systems work and which do not.

---

## Building Management in Standard and Marginal Tiers

Premium buildings have full building management AI integration including leader agents in executive spaces. Standard and marginal residential buildings do not.

Residential buildings below the premium tier run simpler management infrastructure — access control, basic climate, fire suppression, maintenance scheduling. The specialist agent layer without the senate or leader components. Automated and functional but not intelligent in the way premium building management is.

This is a deliberate cost decision rather than a capability gap. Vantage could install full management systems in standard residential buildings. The data return would be valuable. The cost of installation and maintenance at scale across their entire standard portfolio does not currently justify the investment.

The implication is that Vantage's comprehensive biometric data on residents is concentrated in their premium portfolio. Standard residential generates access and movement data but not the physiological detail that premium building pairing produces. Vantage knows a great deal about how their premium residents live. They know significantly less about their standard residents beyond patterns of presence and absence.

---

## The Commissary System

Every Vantage building above marginal tier has commissary access — either an internal commissary within the building or access to a shared commissary serving a cluster of buildings. These commissaries are the primary food retail point for most residents.

Commissary stocking is where the cold war with Clarent is most visible to ordinary residents. In premium buildings the commissary stocks both synthetic lines and genuine GMO imports. In standard buildings the ratio shifts — more synthetic, less premium, the real food present but at prices that make it an occasional purchase rather than a staple. The shelf space allocation, pricing, and availability of premium lines in any given building is a live variable in the Clarent-Vantage negotiation at any given time.

Residents do not experience this as a political conflict. They experience it as their building's commissary sometimes having the good food and sometimes not.
