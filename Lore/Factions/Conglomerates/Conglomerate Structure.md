# Conglomerate Structure

*The PepsiCo model applied to urban governance. Everything is a subsidiary. Everyone wants to become everything.*

---

## The Model

New Halcyon's major power players are conglomerates — holding companies that have swallowed entire sectors of civic and commercial life. The analogy is a present-day company like PepsiCo: a single corporate entity that owns dozens of distinct brands across seemingly unrelated categories. In New Halcyon this logic has extended beyond food and consumer goods into housing, security, water, transit, media, and healthcare.

Small profitable companies get bought out or muscled into subsidiary status. Those that resist either fail or find themselves competing against a conglomerate with vastly greater resources. Over time, the city's economic landscape consolidates upward — many visible brands, a handful of actual power centres.

---

## The Expansion Imperative

Every major conglomerate in New Halcyon is attempting to become everything. This is not merely ambition — it is structural. A conglomerate that controls only one sector is vulnerable to pressure from anyone who controls an adjacent sector. Clarent controls food but depends on Vantage's buildings for retail access. Vantage controls buildings but depends on Ardent's hardware for security. Ardent manufactures augments but lost the residential contracts that gave them leverage over where people lived.

The solution each conglomerate pursues is the same: expand into every sector until no single dependency can be used against you. None have achieved this. All are trying. The cold war between Clarent and Vantage, the grudge Ardent carries from the settlement, the CFA's careful neutrality — all of these are expressions of the same underlying dynamic: everyone is trying to become indispensable to everyone else while simultaneously reducing their own dependencies.

---

## What This Means in Practice

- A resident may pay rent to a housing subsidiary, buy food from a retail subsidiary, receive healthcare from a medical subsidiary, and be protected by a security subdivision — all ultimately owned by the same parent conglomerate, without necessarily knowing it
- Conglomerate brand identity is deliberately obscured at the subsidiary level — street-facing brands feel local and independent; the ownership structure is technically findable but practically invisible
- Switching away from one conglomerate's ecosystem is difficult; the alternatives are often subsidiaries of a rival conglomerate, and the practical differences for ordinary residents are minimal
- Every sector has multiple conglomerate presences, but each conglomerate has a primary sector that reflects their origin and generates their core revenue

## The Four Major Conglomerates

### Clarent Group
Origin sector: food and consumer goods. Primary revenue: synthetic food manufacturing and distribution. See [[Clarent Group]].

### Vantage Group
Origin sector: residential property and building infrastructure. Primary revenue: residential management across New Halcyon's vertical housing stock. See [[Vantage Group]].

### Ardent Systems
Origin sector: weapons manufacturing, augmentation, and security technology. Primary revenue: augment and weapons sales representing approximately 40% of New Halcyon's market. Diminished from pre-War position following settlement. See [[Ardent Systems]].

### Continental Freight Authority
Not a conglomerate in the standard sense — an open infrastructure entity with a manufacturing operation that functions like one. Origin: Country B port and logistics division. Primary revenue: infrastructure taxation on goods moving through CFA channels, supplemented by secret augment manufacturing under the Hariken front company. See [[Continental Freight Authority]].

## Augment and Weapons Market Share

The augment and weapons market is the clearest illustration of how conglomerate competition functions in New Halcyon — multiple significant players, no majority holder, each with specific advantages:

| Party | Approximate Share | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Ardent Systems | ~40% | Largest single manufacturer. Durability philosophy. Full range. |
| Hariken (CFA) | ~30% | Secretly CFA manufactured. Country C front identity. Mid-tier quality. |
| Clarent, Vantage, others | ~30% | Fragmented among smaller manufacturers and conglomerate in-house lines |

The market consistently fails to meet demand. New Halcyon's population growth through CFA import infrastructure means augment demand outpaces supply. Older generation augments never fully leave circulation because replacement supply cannot keep pace. This persistent undersupply sustains all manufacturers regardless of quality differentiation.

## Manufacturer Philosophy

Each manufacturer has a philosophical bias baked into their products regardless of model or tier — a subtle fingerprint that reflects the company's origin and culture:

- **Ardent** — durability and stress tolerance. Hardware that works when conditions stop being clean.
- **Clarent** — accessibility and ease of use. Lower skill ceiling, more intuitive calibration, better defaults.
- **Vantage** — sensory and environmental awareness. Eye, audio, and input augments with superior environmental processing.
- **Hariken/CFA** — system integration and consistency. Components that work well together with low bandwidth overhead, optimised for automated production.

The philosophy is a bias not a fixed characteristic. It influences all products but does not override the model system — a performance model from Clarent is still performance focused, it simply also carries Clarent's accessibility fingerprint.

## Helix Foundry

A pharmaceutical and biological technology company that does not fit the conglomerate expansion model. Helix is not trying to become everything — they are trying to solve a specific problem. This makes them anomalous in New Halcyon's power structure and shapes how every other faction relates to them. See [[Helix Foundry]].
