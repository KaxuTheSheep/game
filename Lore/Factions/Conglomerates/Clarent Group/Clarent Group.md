# Clarent Group

*The Federal States' dinner table. They built it. They own it. They decide what's on it.*

---

## Overview

Clarent Group is the Federal States' dominant food and consumer goods conglomerate — the largest synthetic food manufacturer in New Halcyon, the primary retail distributor of United Territories GMO imports, and the invisible parent of more household brand names than most residents could count. It is not experienced as a single entity by the people who depend on it. It is experienced as whatever brand is on the packaging in their hand.

That invisibility is deliberate and carefully maintained.

---

## Origin

Clarent Group did not begin as Clarent Group. It began as [[Nestlé]] — the Swiss multinational food conglomerate, already one of the largest food companies in the world before [[The Catalyst (2039)]]. Recognising that the Federal States' collapsing regulatory environment represented an unprecedented consolidation opportunity, Nestlé moved aggressively through the 2040s — acquiring failing domestic food brands, absorbing surviving PepsiCo product lines, and restructuring its Federal States operations under a new American-facing parent identity.

Clarent Group was that identity. The name was chosen deliberately: no foreign connotations, no corporate history to inherit, nothing to suggest that the company feeding the Federal States was headquartered anywhere other than here. By the time New Halcyon stabilised, Clarent had deep enough roots that the question of its origin had stopped occurring to anyone.

Nestlé still exists — as one of Clarent's most recognisable grouped brands, sitting comfortably in the mid-tier alongside dozens of others. Most residents have never heard of Clarent Group. Everyone has heard of Nestlé.

---

## Structure — The Roman Senate

Clarent Group is not run by a single executive. It is governed by a internal board — referred to informally within the company as the Senate — composed of senior division heads, major shareholders, and legacy family representatives from the original Nestlé acquisition era. Decision-making is procedural, slow, and deeply political. Factions form around interest blocs rather than ideology.

This structure has one historical exception: during [[The War]], the Senate voted to appoint a wartime executive with consolidated authority — a role with no formal name but referred to internally and eventually historically as the Dictator, in the Roman tradition of the word. That appointment and its consequences are documented in [[The War]].

The Senate returned to full authority after the War. The experience of the Dictator's tenure — and how the War ended for Clarent — left the Senate deeply cautious about concentrated power. Decisions now move slower than ever.

## Primary Sectors

**Synthetic Food Manufacturing** — Clarent is the Federal States' largest producer of synthetic food. Their manufacturing facilities operate at city-feeding scale. Their synthetic lines are sold under dozens of brand names at multiple price tiers — all of them micronutrient deficient, some more palatably disguised than others. See [[Clarent - Brands & Subsidiaries]].

**GMO Import Distribution** — Clarent purchases United Territories agricultural imports primarily through [[Continental Freight Authority]] logistics channels and distributes them under premium brand lines. This is their highest-margin product category and their most visible class marker — the same conglomerate that sells you deficient synthetic food also sells you the real food that fixes what the synthetic food does to you, at a price most residents cannot afford.

**Retail & Consumer Goods** — through their subsidiary and franchise network, Clarent controls a significant portion of New Halcyon's food retail footprint. Street-level vendors, district markets, building commissaries — many are Clarent franchises without displaying Clarent branding.

**Military & Enforcement** — see [[Clarent - Military & Enforcement]].

## The War & Its Consequences

Clarent entered [[The War]] with what seemed like an overwhelming strategic weapon: they were the city's primary food supplier. The Senate — under the appointed Dictator — authorised a full economic withdrawal, removing Clarent from the food supply chain to starve out their opponents.

The strategy failed. Competitor conglomerates bought or seized remaining stock. Ordinary residents died. The War dragged on. Clarent haemorrhaged money from lost income and military upkeep simultaneously — a position they had not modelled adequately before committing to the strategy.

The consequences shaped everything that followed:

- Clarent emerged from the War financially weakened relative to the other majors
- The food embargo is remembered in New Halcyon. Not loudly, not politically — but vendors know, residents know, and it sits underneath every Clarent interaction as a background fact
- [[Continental Freight Authority]] moved into the UT import gap Clarent created during the withdrawal and never fully gave it back
- The Senate retired the Dictator model and has never reappointed one

Clarent's current use of military force — targeted, local, commercially motivated — is the direct lesson of the War. They learned not that force was wrong, but that force must generate income rather than cost it.

## Philosophy

*"From the ground up, for the long haul. Clarent Group feeds the nation — today, tomorrow, and for the generations that come after. We believe that access to food is the foundation of everything else: community, productivity, progress. That's not a mission statement. That's a promise."*

The language is frontier-coded and warm. It implies rootedness, reliability, patriotism. It does not mention that the food it sells at scale knowingly falls short of full nutrition, that the solution to that shortfall is sold at a separate premium price point, or that the additive introduced into its synthetic lines in 2050 has measurably affected the cognitive capacity of a generation of New Halcyon residents. See [[Clarent - Haze]].

Internally: purely performative at the top. The Senate does not believe the promise. Approximately 40% of mid-to-lower management genuinely do — they have absorbed the language fully enough that they experience themselves as people doing important work. This is not accidental.

---

## Position in New Halcyon

Clarent is everywhere and nowhere simultaneously. Its brands are on every shelf, in every commissary, in every building vending system. Its parent name appears on almost nothing a resident would encounter in daily life.

Its cold war position — between the two stalemate majors — is complicated by the War legacy. Clarent has leverage through food distribution but everyone remembers that leverage failed once. They deploy it carefully now, preferring franchise coercion and military pressure at the vendor level over anything that looks like a city-wide strategic play.
