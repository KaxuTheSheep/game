# Haze

*Street name for Prolamine-7. What it does, when it started, and what Clarent knows.*

---

## What It Is

Prolamine-7 is a synthetic binding compound introduced into Clarent Group's mid-to-lower synthetic food lines in 2050 — approximately a decade before [[The War]]. Its official function, as documented in Clarent's internal manufacturing records, is as a flavour-stabilising agent: it extends shelf life, improves texture consistency across production batches, and marginally reduces per-unit manufacturing cost.

That is what it does. It is not why it was introduced.

---

## What It Actually Does

Prolamine-7 increases consumption frequency. It achieves this through two mechanisms:

**Appetite cycling** — it shortens the satiation window, meaning food containing it satisfies hunger for less time than equivalent caloric content without it. Consumers eat more often. They buy more product. The effect is subtle enough that nobody identifies it as a cause — they simply feel hungry sooner and eat accordingly.

**Mild neurological binding** — at cumulative exposure levels reached through regular long-term consumption, Prolamine-7 produces a low-grade dependency response. Not addiction in any dramatic sense. A baseline preference for the foods containing it, a slight discomfort when they are absent, a mild cognitive fog in their absence that resolves on consumption. Functionally invisible at the individual level. Statistically significant across a population.

The cognitive fog in withdrawal is where the name comes from. *Haze* — street-level shorthand for the dull, slightly-off feeling that residents in poorer districts notice without being able to attribute it. Most assume it is a symptom of synthetic food deficiency generally. Some are correct in a narrow sense without being correct about the cause.

---

## The 2050 Research

Clarent's internal research division — a subsidiary operating under a name with no visible Clarent connection — produced a longitudinal neurological study in 2050 tracking Prolamine-7's effects across a test population over eight years. The study was commissioned to confirm what internal researchers already suspected: that long-term Prolamine-7 exposure at population scale would produce measurable cognitive effects.

It confirmed this. Specifically:

- Individuals with ten or more years of regular Prolamine-7 exposure show statistically significant IQ reduction relative to control groups on equivalent synthetic diets without the compound
- The effect is most pronounced in individuals whose diet is exclusively synthetic — i.e. the poorest residents with no access to UT imports or premium lines, who are also the highest per-capita consumers of the affected product lines
- The effect compounds generationally — children born to high-exposure parents show baseline cognitive differences before any personal exposure

The study was classified internally. It does not officially exist. The researchers involved were absorbed into other Clarent divisions or departed the company under terms that included non-disclosure provisions.

---

## What Clarent Did With It

Nothing visible. The Senate reviewed the research findings. The consensus — never formally minuted — was that Prolamine-7's commercial value outweighed the liability risk, that the neurological effects were sufficiently gradual and diffuse to resist attribution, and that the existing narrative around synthetic food deficiency provided adequate cover for any population-level cognitive trends that became statistically observable.

The compound remained in the product lines. The study remained buried.

What changed: Clarent's premium lines — sold under Nestlé and Meridian Foods branding to middle and upper-tier residents — were quietly reformulated to reduce Prolamine-7 content. The compound is still present in trace amounts for flavour consistency, but below the threshold at which neurological effects accumulate meaningfully.

The people who can afford to eat better are, without knowing it, also protected from this specific harm. The people who cannot are not.

---

## Present Day

By the 2100s setting, the 2050 cohort — residents who were in their twenties and thirties when Prolamine-7 was introduced — are now in their seventies. The cognitive effects are visible in population health data as a statistical anomaly: a measurable IQ reduction in that generation relative to both predecessors and successors, concentrated in lower-income districts. The data exists. It has been attributed to synthetic food deficiency, environmental factors, and the general health impacts of [[The War]].

No public attribution to Prolamine-7 has ever been made.

Haze as a street term refers now to two things interchangeably: the withdrawal fog that regular consumers notice, and — among factions and individuals who know more — the broader condition of a population that has been quietly made less capable of thinking clearly about its own situation.

## Faction Hooks

**The rising pharmaceutical company** — their micronutrient research has brought them into proximity with Federal States population health data. Whether they have found the Prolamine-7 signal in that data, correctly attributed it, and understood its implications is an open question with significant consequences. If they have, they hold something real on Clarent. What they choose to do with it depends on how much they believe in their own stated mission versus how much they want leverage.

**Commonweal** — operates in the districts with the highest Prolamine-7 exposure. Their community health infrastructure means they are closer to the raw human reality of Haze than any other faction. They may not have the data. They have the people.

**Any faction with access to pre-2050 Clarent internal records** — the research exists somewhere. It was classified, not destroyed. This is a recoverable document in the right hands.
