# Ardent Systems — Augment & Weapons Manufacturing

*40% of the market. Nobody else is close. That is not the same as control.*

---

## Market Position

Ardent holds approximately 40% of New Halcyon's combined augment and weapons market — the largest single manufacturer but not a majority holder. The remaining share is split between CFA imports under Hariken branding (~30%), Clarent, Vantage, and smaller manufacturers (~30% combined).

The fragmentation is partly natural market competition and partly deliberate competitor strategy. Clarent and Vantage have both invested in augment manufacturing to reduce Ardent dependency. Neither produces Ardent-quality hardware across the full range. Both produce augments with specific strengths that reflect their company philosophies and general weaknesses compared to Ardent's more comprehensive lines.

---

## Manufacturing Philosophy

**Durability and stress tolerance** is the bias baked into everything Ardent makes regardless of model or tier. Ardent augments are designed to function under conditions that degrade competitor hardware — sustained combat use, poor maintenance environments, physical trauma, power fluctuation. They are not always the highest performing augments in clean conditions. They are the augments that still work when conditions stop being clean.

This philosophy reflects Ardent's military origin and their War experience. Hardware that fails under stress is not acceptable. The lesson was learned at cost and is now institutional.

The manufacturing philosophy is a bias not a fixed characteristic — it influences all products but does not override the model system. An Ardent ease-of-use model is designed for beginners but is more durable than a competitor's equivalent ease-of-use model. An Ardent performance model demands operator skill but survives punishment that would destroy alternatives.

---

## The Model System

Within each augment type Ardent produces multiple models representing different design priorities:

**Performance models** — maximum capability ceiling, demanding to operate, assumes experienced users who will push the hardware. These are the augments serious combat operators want if they have the skill to use them. High bandwidth draw, fast response, unforgiving of operator error.

**Ease of use models** — lower skill ceiling, more intuitive NI calibration, better default settings. Ardent's beginner-friendly lines have better durability than any competitor's equivalent tier because the philosophy applies regardless of the model's intended user. A civilian running an Ardent ease-of-use arm augment gets hardware that will outlast what they paid for.

**Balanced models** — neither extreme. The most commercially significant category because it serves the widest market. Where Ardent's durability philosophy is most visible — balanced models from competitors are often notably less robust than equivalent balanced models from Ardent even at the same price point.

---

## SOMA Public Lines vs Proprietary Military

Ardent manufactures both:

**SOMA compatible public lines** — the 40% market share product. Available through standard commercial channels, fitter networks, corporate health packages, and grey market. All standard augment categories. Priced across multiple tiers to serve the full market from budget to premium.

**Proprietary military framework augments** — exclusively for Ardent's own Tier 1 force and specific contracted partners. Not commercially available. Built to Ardent's proprietary framework specifications which are incompatible with SOMA. The capability gap between Ardent's public SOMA lines and their proprietary military hardware is something Ardent does not discuss publicly. The gap exists.

The decision to maintain two separate product lines rather than making their best hardware commercially available is partly commercial — they could charge more for it — and partly strategic. Their best hardware running inside their enemies is a liability.

## Weapons

Ardent's weapons manufacturing covers the full spectrum from standard civilian firearms through military grade hardware. Their public weapons lines follow the same durability philosophy as their augments — Ardent weapons are not always the most technically sophisticated but they function reliably in conditions where more complex systems fail.

Caseless ammunition is standard across their mid to high tier weapons lines. Their electronic firing mechanisms are designed with redundancy — backup mechanical systems that engage if the electronic primary fails. This is the durability philosophy applied to weapons: a weapon that fails to fire at the critical moment is unacceptable, therefore multiple failure modes are designed out rather than assumed acceptable.

Their military proprietary lines include railgun configurations and directed energy weapons not available in their commercial catalogue. These are produced for their own Tier 1 force and have appeared in the grey market occasionally — War-era hardware that survived the settlement and found its way into militia and independent operator hands. Greyback Company almost certainly has examples.

---

## The Vulnerability Question

Ardent knows every edge case in the public SOMA augments they manufacture. They designed them. They know where the hardware fails under specific conditions, what the NI write channel tolerances actually are before the limiter engages, and what would happen to someone running their augments if specific parameters were pushed in specific ways.

This knowledge has never been publicly deployed as an attack. It exists. The awareness that it exists is itself a form of leverage — any faction running Ardent augments and thinking carefully about their security situation has to account for the possibility that their hardware manufacturer could, if motivated, identify vulnerabilities in their specific augment configuration faster than they could patch them.

Ardent has not done this. Whether that restraint reflects ethics, strategic calculation, or the recognition that using it once would destroy their commercial position permanently is not something Ardent has clarified.

---

## Grey Market Presence

Ardent hardware has significant grey market presence in New Halcyon's forgotten districts. War-era augments and weapons that survived the settlement and subsequent decades of use circulate through salvage networks, independent fitters, and direct sale between former soldiers and current operators.

This grey market Ardent hardware is old, often poorly maintained, and sometimes modified in ways that compromise the original design integrity. It is also often the most capable hardware available in forgotten districts precisely because Ardent's durability philosophy means decades-old hardware still functions at levels that current budget commercial alternatives cannot match.

The grey market existence is something Ardent neither encourages nor actively suppresses. They do not support or warranty grey market hardware. They also recognise that the reputation built by War-era hardware still functioning in forgotten districts decades later is a form of marketing that money cannot buy.
