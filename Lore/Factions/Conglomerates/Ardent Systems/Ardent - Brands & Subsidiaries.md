# Ardent Systems — Brands & Subsidiaries

*Less brand architecture than Clarent or Vantage. Ardent does not hide what it is as carefully as they do.*

---

## Structure

Ardent's brand structure is simpler than Clarent's or Vantage's — partly because they lost significant subsidiary infrastructure in the post-War settlement, partly because their culture does not invest heavily in the performance of invisibility that the other conglomerates maintain. Ardent products tend to carry Ardent adjacent branding rather than fully independent identities. People who buy Ardent augments often know they are buying Ardent augments.

This is not entirely unintentional. Ardent's durability reputation is a commercial asset. Obscuring the parent brand would cost more than it gained.

---

## Primary Subsidiaries

**Ardent Defence**
The weapons manufacturing arm. Produces the full commercial weapons catalogue under the Ardent Defence name — the one subsidiary where the parent company name is explicitly present in the brand. Civilian and commercial weapons sales. Military hardware sales to Tier 1 forces and contracted partners. The brand is recognised at street level and carries the durability reputation that drives purchasing decisions.

**Ironframe Augmentation**
Ardent's commercial augment line sold under a subsidiary name rather than directly as Ardent. The separation is partial — Ironframe is known in the market to be Ardent's augment brand, but the subsidiary structure allows different pricing, warranty terms, and commercial relationships than the parent company maintains. Ironframe augments are what fitters stock and what corporate health packages include. The Ardent proprietary military lines do not carry this branding.

**Bastion Security Solutions**
Ardent's commercial security services subsidiary. Provides security hardware, personnel training, and contracted security services to the Tier 2 market and commercial clients who want Ardent-quality equipment without a direct Ardent relationship. Bastion is how Ardent maintains a footprint in the residential security space that the post-War settlement stripped from the parent company — they cannot hold the contracts directly, but Bastion hardware and Bastion-trained operators are present throughout the Tier 2 ecosystem.

Vantage is Bastion's largest single client. The irony of Ardent's security subsidiary being the primary hardware supplier to the conglomerate that absorbed Ardent's residential contracts is not lost on Ardent's leadership. It is also the closest thing to a foothold inside Vantage's infrastructure that Ardent currently has.

**Ardent Medical**
The subsidiary nobody prioritised acquiring in the post-War settlement. Operates in pharmaceutical research, medical augmentation, and biological interface technology. Currently the connection point for Ardent's relationship with Helix Foundry — a commercially defensible medical research partnership that provides cover for Ardent's interest in Helix's animal framework technology.

Ardent Medical's significance has grown substantially since the settlement. What looked like a minor research division has become Ardent's most strategically interesting subsidiary, which is something Clarent and Vantage are quietly revising their assessment of.

---

## Lost Subsidiaries

Several significant Ardent subsidiaries were stripped in the post-War settlement and now operate under other ownership:

**Consumer goods lines** — absorbed by Clarent. Several brands that residents currently experience as Clarent subsidiaries were built under Ardent ownership. Clarent rebranded them or folded them into existing grouped brand structures. The original Ardent identity is gone from these products.

**Residential management subsidiary** — absorbed by Vantage. The specific subsidiary name is pending design but its absorption is what gave Vantage several of the building contracts that form the foundation of their current residential portfolio.

**Logistics subsidiary** — absorbed by the CFA during the War's later period. The CFA's presence in New Halcyon's internal logistics network is partly built on infrastructure that was originally Ardent's.

These losses are documented internally at Ardent as a record of what the settlement cost rather than as active grievances — though the distinction between documentation and grievance is thinner than Ardent's leadership would publicly acknowledge.
