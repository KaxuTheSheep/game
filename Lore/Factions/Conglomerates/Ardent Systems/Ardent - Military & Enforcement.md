# Ardent Systems — Military & Enforcement

*The best equipped Tier 1 force in New Halcyon. Deployed the least. Both facts are intentional.*

---

## Overview

Ardent's Tier 1 force is probably the most capable military unit in New Halcyon in terms of hardware quality and personnel training. They manufacture the weapons and augments their own force runs — proprietary military framework augments not available in their commercial catalogue, weapons systems that do not appear in civilian or competitor hands. Their people are trained to a standard that reflects a company whose institutional memory includes fighting and losing a war, and understanding precisely what that cost.

The force is deployed rarely. This is the lesson of the War applied to military doctrine: force that costs more than it recovers is a liability. Every deployment is evaluated on whether it serves Ardent's recovery strategy. Engagements that do not advance that strategy do not happen.

---

## Proprietary Framework Force

Ardent's military personnel run Ardent's proprietary framework rather than SOMA. This is a significant operational security decision — their personnel cannot be hacked through SOMA attack vectors, their augment configurations cannot be mapped by anyone without access to Ardent's proprietary specifications, and their hardware cannot be compromised through the standard injection attack channels that affect SOMA-based opponents.

The cost is the standard proprietary framework tradeoff — incompatibility with the SOMA augment ecosystem means Ardent's military personnel are entirely dependent on Ardent's own supply chain for augment maintenance, replacement, and upgrade. This is acceptable for a force that manufactures its own hardware. It would be a critical vulnerability for anyone else.

---

## Deployment Doctrine

**What Ardent deploys force for:**
- Protection of manufacturing facilities and research infrastructure
- Security for high value personnel and assets
- Situations where Ardent's commercial or recovery interests are directly threatened
- Demonstrating capability without deploying it — the visible presence of Ardent military hardware in a negotiation communicates without requiring action

**What Ardent does not deploy force for:**
- Territory disputes that do not affect their manufacturing or recovery strategy
- Conflicts between other conglomerates where neutrality preserves their hardware supply relationships with both sides
- Situations where the commercial cost of involvement exceeds the strategic benefit

This doctrine is explicit internally and understood externally. Ardent's force is not for hire and does not take sides in conflicts between their commercial partners. This restraint is simultaneously principled and commercial — they sell to Clarent, Vantage, and the Tier 2 firms. Taking sides destroys those relationships.

---

## Relationship With Their Own Hardware

Ardent's military personnel have access to hardware that their commercial customers do not. The gap between Ardent's public SOMA lines and their proprietary military augments is real and significant. Personnel who have run both describe the proprietary hardware as feeling more integrated — less like wearing equipment and more like using your own body, because the proprietary framework's tighter specifications allow calibration that SOMA's open standard architecture cannot match.

This creates a specific dynamic within the force: Ardent's soldiers know they are running the best hardware their company produces. They also know their company's commercial customers are running hardware that is deliberately less capable. The institutional awareness that the company makes two tiers of product and keeps the better one for itself is not a secret inside Ardent. It is a source of professional pride that occasionally manifests as contempt for operators running commercial lines.

---

## Post-War Rebuilding

Ardent's force was significantly reduced during and after the War — personnel losses, equipment destroyed, and the financial constraints of the post-War settlement limiting rebuilding capacity. The current force is smaller than the pre-War peak and has been rebuilding slowly since.

The rebuilding prioritises quality over quantity. Fewer personnel running better hardware with more thorough training produces a force that projects capability disproportionate to its size. The visible presence of a small Ardent unit with proprietary framework augments and military-grade weapons communicates more than a larger force with commercial hardware.

This is also the only rebuilding approach Ardent can currently afford. The settlement stripped the revenue streams that funded their pre-War force scale. Until the recovery strategy produces new revenue, the force stays small and capable rather than large and under-equipped.

## Greyback Company

The relationship between Ardent and Greyback Company is complicated by history in ways that neither party has fully resolved.

Greyback Company was founded by former Ardent Tier 1 soldiers who left after the War. They took with them Ardent training, Ardent institutional knowledge, and in many cases Ardent hardware — War-era equipment that was technically Ardent property and whose departure Ardent chose not to pursue, calculating that a public dispute over equipment with their own veterans was a worse outcome than the loss.

Ardent's current leadership views Greyback with a mixture of respect and wariness. Respect because they are trained Ardent personnel who have continued to operate at a professional standard. Wariness because they are trained Ardent personnel operating outside Ardent's control, carrying institutional knowledge of Ardent's military doctrine and hardware vulnerabilities that would be valuable to Ardent's competitors.

Neither party has moved against the other. The implicit understanding is that Greyback stays in their lane and Ardent does not pursue the hardware question. Whether that understanding holds as Ardent's recovery strategy advances and Greyback's operations continue to expand is an open question.
