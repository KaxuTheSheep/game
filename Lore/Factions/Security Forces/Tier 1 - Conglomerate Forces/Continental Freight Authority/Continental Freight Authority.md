# Continental Freight Authority (CFA)

*Country B's former port division. Present in New Halcyon since the founding period. Nobody knows who it answers to now.*

---

## What It Is

The Continental Freight Authority began as a state-backed port and logistics division of [[Country B]], established in New Halcyon during the city's founding period as part of Country B's strategy for maintaining economic presence inside the Federal States' most significant privatised city. It handled goods flows, port operations, and logistics infrastructure — and brought its own security apparatus with it.

At some point — the exact moment is disputed and possibly unknowable — the CFA's relationship with Country B's state became ambiguous. It may still answer to Country B. It may have been cut loose. It may be both simultaneously: formally independent, quietly still reporting. Nobody outside the CFA knows. It is possible nobody inside fully knows either.

---

## Public Knowledge vs. Unknown

**Known:** The CFA originated as a Country B state division. Its personnel, infrastructure, and early funding came from Country B. It has been present in New Halcyon since the founding era.

**Unknown:** Whether it currently receives instructions, funding, or strategic direction from Country B's government. Whether its leadership considers itself a Country B asset or an independent entity. What it would do if Country B's interests and its own New Halcyon position came into direct conflict.

---

## How Other Factions Treat It

With careful neutrality. Attacking or overtly competing with the CFA risks provoking Country B if the backing is still active. Treating it as fully independent and ignoring its potential state connection is naive. Every other Tier 1 force, every conglomerate, and every external actor in New Halcyon has arrived at the same uneasy middle position: maintain working relations, avoid direct confrontation, and never fully trust it.

The CFA does not discourage this ambiguity. It may actively cultivate it.

---

## Operations

Logistics, freight, port infrastructure, and associated security in New Halcyon. Commercially viable as a standalone operation — it does not need Country B's backing to function economically, which is part of what makes the question of its loyalty so difficult to resolve. It could be independent. It would look exactly the same if it weren't.
