# Timeline

Chronological spine of the world from present day to the 2100s setting. For individual named events see [[The Catalyst (2039)]] and [[The War]].

---

## 2026–2038 — Erosion

Status quo degradation. No single dramatic break — a slow accumulation of systemic failures.

- Automation-driven unemployment hollows out the working and middle classes across the developed world
- Climate strain drives resource competition; infrastructure investment declines in favour of short-term corporate returns
- Regulatory capture accelerates — governments increasingly outsource core functions to private firms under the language of "efficiency"
- Tax bases shrink as corporations restructure faster than states can legislate
- [[Country B]] tightens central state control in direct contrast to the western trajectory
- [[Country C]] begins aggressive automation and AI-labour programmes in response to accelerating demographic collapse

---

## 2039 — The Break

See [[The Catalyst (2039)]].

The Federal States' federal government collapses under compounding debt and an AI-driven market crash. Core state functions are sold to domestic megacorporations as bailout conditions. The state is not overthrown — it is foreclosed on.

Simultaneously, the western and midland states refuse to accept the terms of the sell-off and break away, eventually forming [[The United Territories]].

---

## 2040s–2060s — Fragmentation

- Federal authority in [[The Federal States]] becomes nominal; individual cities auction off services piecemeal
- Cities 1 through X — early attempts at fully privatised urban governance across the Federal States
- Most fail. **New Halcyon** survives and stabilises; it becomes the proof-of-concept others either emulate or fear
- [[The War]] occurs during this window — conglomerate forces fight an escalating series of disputes that nearly destroys New Halcyon before settling into the present-day turf arrangement
- [[Country B]]'s [[Continental Freight Authority]] establishes itself in New Halcyon during the later part of this period
- [[Country F]] begins pocketed SEZ-style privatisation, partly modelled on New Halcyon, partly seeded by returning diaspora from Country A's failed cities

---

## 2060s–2080s — Consolidation

- The Federal States is now a patchwork: fully privatised zones like New Halcyon, nominally governed but poorer regions, and ungoverned transitional areas
- [[Country B]] and [[Country D]] begin projecting influence into the Federal States' power vacuum — not through direct state action but through proxy conglomerate investment and covert interference
- [[The United Territories]] consolidates its agricultural-industrial base; GMO agricultural factories reach full operational scale
- The Federal States begins synthetic food development in response to growing food dependency — see [[Synthetic Food Tech]]

---

## 2080s–2100s — Present Day

The setting of the game. New Halcyon is multiple generations deep into statelessness. Nobody alive remembers functioning government as a lived experience — it is history, not memory.

- [[The Reserve]] continues eroding credibility; crypto and closed-community currencies are mainstream in poorer districts
- Micronutrient deficiency from synthetic food is a visible class divide — see [[Synthetic Food]] and [[GMO Agricultural Imports]]
- The Federal States' micronutrient pill programme exists but is nowhere near population scale — see [[Micronutrient Research]]
- The A/G food dependency tension is a live background condition; certain factions and NPCs actively push toward confrontation
