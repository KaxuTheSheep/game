# The War

The inter-conglomerate conflict that nearly destroyed New Halcyon before it had properly established itself. Not planned, not declared — accumulated. Three parties who each believed they could win outright. None of them did.

---

## What It Was

A series of small, individually unremarkable disputes between tier-1 conglomerate security forces that escalated progressively because no state mechanism existed to de-escalate them. Nobody started a war. Everyone started something smaller. The war is what those smaller things became.

The three primary parties — Ardent Systems, Clarent Group, and Vantage Group — entered the conflict with different capabilities, different objectives, and different assumptions about how it would resolve. All three assumptions were wrong.

---

## When

During the 2040s–2060s founding period of New Halcyon — after the conglomerates had established real power and territory, but before the city had stabilised into its present-day arrangement.

---

## The Three-Way Structure

**Ardent's position:**
Ardent entered the War as the most militarily capable faction — they manufactured the weapons and augments, they had the largest and best-equipped Tier 1 force, and they had the broadest sector coverage of any single conglomerate. Their objective was consolidation — a single dominant conglomerate controlling New Halcyon, which they believed their military advantage made achievable.

The central irony of Ardent's position: every faction fighting them was using Ardent hardware. The weapons that damaged Ardent's forces were Ardent weapons. The augments running in the soldiers attacking Ardent positions were Ardent augments. They had built the instruments of their own diminishment.

**Clarent's position:**
Clarent controlled food distribution and used it as a strategic weapon — the Senate appointed a wartime executive, referred to internally as the Dictator in the Roman tradition, with consolidated authority to execute a full economic withdrawal. Removing Clarent from the food supply chain was intended to starve out opponents financially and create civilian pressure that would force settlement on Clarent's terms.

The strategy failed. Competitor conglomerates bought or seized remaining food stock. Ordinary residents died. The War dragged on. Clarent haemorrhaged money from lost income and military upkeep simultaneously — a position they had not modelled adequately before committing to the strategy.

**Vantage's position:**
Vantage entered the War as the least militarily capable of the three but with a structural advantage — they owned the buildings everyone else needed to operate inside. Their strategy was endurance rather than victory: maintain operational capability long enough for the other parties to exhaust themselves, then negotiate from a position of relative strength.

---

## The Alliance Shift

The War's defining turning point was not a battle. It was a calculation.

Ardent's consolidation objective made them a threat to both Clarent and Vantage in a way that their conflict with each other did not. Both Clarent and Vantage separately reached the same conclusion: Ardent winning was worse for each of them than the status quo of fighting each other. The alliance that formed was not friendly and not formal — it was two parties independently prioritising the same enemy.

The combined pressure of Clarent and Vantage against Ardent's military capability did not produce a clean victory. Ardent's hardware advantage meant the conflict ground toward exhaustion rather than resolution. All three factions were haemorrhaging money. New Halcyon's stability — the thing they were all fighting over — was deteriorating in ways that reduced the value of winning.

---

## The End

The War did not end with a decisive engagement. It ended gradually, then by agreement.

Each party's financial reality made continuation impossible to justify. Ardent was weakest — the alliance pressure and the cost of fighting two opponents simultaneously had depleted their resources faster than either opponent alone. Clarent had destroyed its own revenue stream through the food embargo and was rebuilding from a position of significant financial damage. Vantage had endured but not without cost.

The ceasefire came informally first — engagements becoming less frequent as all parties quietly reduced operational tempo without formally acknowledging they were doing so. The settlement came after, negotiated when all parties had accepted that the War's outcome would not match any of their original objectives.

---

## The Settlement

The settlement was structured during Ardent's weakest period, which meant Clarent and Vantage had disproportionate influence over its terms.

**What Ardent lost:**
- Residential management and building infrastructure contracts — absorbed by Vantage
- Consumer goods subsidiaries — absorbed by Clarent, rebranded or folded into existing brand structures
- Logistics subsidiary — absorbed by the CFA, which had established itself during the War's later period
- Residual security contracts stripped into the Tier 2 market as a compromise measure

**What Ardent kept:**
- Weapons manufacturing and augmentation lines
- Military hardware and their Tier 1 force, diminished but intact
- Medical subsidiary — overlooked by both Clarent and Vantage as apparently minor at the time

**What Clarent lost:**
- The Dictator model was retired — the Senate returned to full authority and has never reappointed a consolidated executive
- Significant financial reserves depleted by the dual cost of the food embargo's lost revenue and continued military upkeep
- The food embargo is remembered in New Halcyon. Not loudly, not politically — but it sits underneath every Clarent interaction as a background fact that vendors and long-term residents carry

**What Vantage gained:**
- Ardent's residential management and building infrastructure contracts — the foundation of their current portfolio
- Post-War positioning as the least damaged of the three major parties, which translated into disproportionate influence over the settlement terms relative to their military contribution

**The CFA's emergence:**
The Continental Freight Authority established itself in New Halcyon during the War's later period with Country B's backing. The chaos of the War created gaps in logistics and port infrastructure that the CFA moved into. By the time the settlement was negotiated the CFA was sufficiently established that absorbing Ardent's logistics subsidiary was a natural extension of existing operations rather than a new acquisition. The other conglomerates accepted this because disputing it meant disputing an entity with Country B's implicit backing while still recovering from the War — a confrontation none of them wanted.

---

## What It Means Now

- Present-day turf boundaries, alliances, and grudges in New Halcyon trace directly back to the War's settlement
- Clarent's institutional caution about deploying food supply as a strategic weapon comes from the Dictator strategy's failure
- Ardent's bitter institutional culture and long-term recovery strategy are direct products of what the settlement cost them
- Vantage's post-War expansion is the foundation of their current residential portfolio
- [[Greyback Company]] is a direct product of the War — its founder walked away from Ardent's Tier 1 force in the aftermath, taking institutional knowledge and War-era hardware with them
- The CFA's position in New Halcyon is structurally rooted in the opportunity the War created
