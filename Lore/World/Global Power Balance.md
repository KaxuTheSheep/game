# Global Power Balance
Present-day (2100s) summary of how the major nations relate to each other and to New Halcyon.

| Nation | Type | Strength | Weakness | Role |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| [[The Federal States]] | Fragmented corporate-administered patchwork | Financial, industrial, urban density | Food dependency, internal inequality, no central authority | Home of New Halcyon; the setting |
| [[The United Territories]] | Collectivist agrarian-tech state | Food production, bio/renewable tech, West Coast scientific base | Militarily weak | Food dependency lever; background tension with Federal States |
| [[Country B]] | Authoritarian-capitalist centralised state | Economic scale, manufacturing, state coordination | Rigidity, internal dissent suppression | External pressure on New Halcyon via [[Continental Freight Authority]]; proxy conglomerate investments |
| [[Country C]] | Automated-labour tech state, quasi-neutral | AI and automation technology, hardware | Demographic fragility, dependency on external markets | Supplies automation tech and AI labour to New Halcyon's conglomerates |
| [[Country D]] | Regulated democratic bloc | Stability, institutional credibility, quality of life | Stagnation, slow adaptation, insular | Moral and diplomatic foil; destination for dissidents fleeing New Halcyon |
| [[Country E]] | Decentralised communal tech-economy | Independent path, not dependent on either model | Smaller global footprint, vulnerable to external economic pressure | "What if it went differently" contrast; possible waypoint for Federal States diaspora |
| [[Country F]] | Two-track state — SEZ privatisation alongside functioning broader government | Scale, technical workforce, uneven but real domestic stability | Internal inequality, tension between SEZ and non-SEZ populations | Second major adopter of the New Halcyon model in pockets; watching the A/G tension closely |

## Key Tensions (Present Day)
- **Federal States vs United Territories** — food dependency makes open conflict mutually destructive but the pressure is building; certain factions in New Halcyon actively want war
- **Country B's ambiguous position in New Halcyon** — the [[Continental Freight Authority]]'s true current loyalty (to Country B, or fully independent) is unknown to all parties; everyone hedges accordingly
- **Country C's neutrality** — sells automation tech to whoever pays, including both sides of the Federal/Territories split; nobody fully trusts it, nobody can afford to cut it off
- **Country D as a pressure valve** — its willingness to accept Federal States refugees and dissidents keeps internal pressure in New Halcyon from boiling over completely; some conglomerates quietly fund this as a social stability measure
