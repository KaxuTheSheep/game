# Country D

*EU-analogue. Regulated democratic bloc. Moral and diplomatic foil to New Halcyon.*

---

## Overview

Country D went the opposite direction to the Federal States. Where Country A deregulated and sold itself off, Country D doubled down on institutional governance, social infrastructure, and corporate regulation — explicitly in response to watching the Federal States' collapse unfold in real time. It is stable, it is relatively equitable, and by the 2100s it is often described as stagnant.

It is not exciting. It works. For most of its citizens that is enough.

---

## Role Relative to New Halcyon

Country D functions as the setting's moral counterweight — proof that the Federal States' trajectory was a choice, not an inevitability. It accepts refugees and dissidents from New Halcyon and similar privatised zones, which quietly serves as a pressure valve preventing total internal collapse in cities like New Halcyon.

Some of New Halcyon's conglomerates quietly fund emigration pathways to Country D as a social stability measure — cheaper than managing a fully radicalised underclass, and deniable.

Country D's diplomatic relationship with the Federal States is cold but functional. It issues regular condemnations of conditions in New Halcyon that nobody in New Halcyon pays attention to.
