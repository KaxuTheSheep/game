# Country D

*EU-analogue. Regulated democratic bloc. Moral foil. First stop on the emigration pipeline.*

---

## Overview

Country D went the opposite direction to the Federal States. Where Country A deregulated and sold itself off, Country D doubled down on institutional governance, social infrastructure, and corporate regulation — explicitly in response to watching the Federal States' trajectory unfold in real time. It is stable, relatively equitable, and by the 2100s is often described as stagnant.

It is not exciting. It works. For most of its citizens that is enough.

---

## Role as Emigration Destination

Country D is the most accessible first destination for Federal States dissidents and refugees. Unlike the [[The United Territories|United Territories]], it does not require biological reconstruction as a precondition for entry. Unlike staying in New Halcyon, it offers a functioning social infrastructure and legal protections.

### SOMA Infrastructure

Country D maintains operational SOMA infrastructure — technicians, support systems, and supply chains capable of servicing Framework-augmented individuals. This is a deliberate policy choice: Country D wants to be able to receive FS refugees without turning them away on medical grounds, and an augmented person who loses Framework support on arrival is a humanitarian and political liability.

This does not mean Country D can do everything the Federal States can. Its SOMA infrastructure is maintenance-oriented rather than cutting-edge — sufficient to keep existing augments functional, not to perform complex new augmentation or cutting-edge modifications.

### Limitations

Country D cannot perform full biological reconstruction. It lacks the United Territories' biotech depth and production capacity. A defector who arrives in Country D still Framework-dependent is stable but not free — they remain augmented, with all the subscription and maintenance implications that entails, until longer-term arrangements (usually involving UT-provided biological components, either through official UT intake or black market supply chains) can be made.

---

## The Conglomerate Angle

Some New Halcyon conglomerates quietly fund emigration pathways to Country D for specific individuals — radicalised workers, troublesome dissidents, people whose presence creates more political cost than their productivity justifies. Removing them to Country D is cheaper than managing them.

This is not public policy and is never formally acknowledged. It operates as a practical arrangement between conglomerate HR functions and various intermediary services.

---

## Diplomatic Position

Country D's relationship with the Federal States is cold but functional. It issues regular public statements about conditions in New Halcyon and similar privatised zones that nobody in New Halcyon pays meaningful attention to. It maintains diplomatic channels because both sides find them occasionally useful.

It is on better terms with the United Territories — shared commitment to functioning governance creates a natural alignment — but not formally allied.
