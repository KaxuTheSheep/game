# Country E

*Nigeria-analogue. Decentralised communal tech-economy. The road not taken.*

---

## Overview

Country E did not follow either dominant model — neither the Federal States' corporate anarchy nor Country B's authoritarian capitalism. Drawing on a pre-existing tradition of distributed community organisation and an early lead in decentralised technology infrastructure, it developed its own path: a communal tech-economy built on distributed systems, local governance structures, and collective resource management.

It is not a utopia. It has its own instabilities, its own inequalities, its own internal disputes. But it is genuinely different from everything else in the world, which makes it the setting's most important "what if" — proof that neither the corporate nor the authoritarian model was the only available response to the pressures of the 2030s–2040s.

---

## Role Relative to New Halcyon

Country E was likely an early waypoint for diaspora fleeing Country A's failed cities (Cities 1 through X) — some of whom eventually carried the New Halcyon model onward to Country F, others of whom stayed and shaped what Country E became.

Its decentralised currency and closed-community economic models have influenced the alternative currency movements growing in New Halcyon's forgotten districts. The connection is cultural and technological rather than political.

---

## Afrofuturist Aesthetic Note

Country E draws on a well-established real-world creative tradition — Lagos-rooted Afrofuturism, Nollywood sci-fi, the genuine current trajectory of West African tech hubs — and should feel like a natural extrapolation of that rather than an imposed external aesthetic.
