# Country F

*India-analogue. Two-track society — SEZ privatisation pockets alongside a functioning broader state.*

---

## Overview

Country F never went all-in on either model. The broader state persisted — democratic, bureaucratic, uneven — while special economic zones in major urban centres adopted full or partial privatisation, partly modelled on New Halcyon, partly developed independently. The result is a country running two parallel systems simultaneously: functioning government in most of its territory, corporate-administered pockets in others.

This makes Country F uniquely unstable in a specific way — the tension between its two tracks is a permanent internal fault line. SEZ populations and non-SEZ populations have diverging material conditions, legal rights, and cultural identities despite nominally being the same country.

---

## Role Relative to New Halcyon

Country F is the second major adopter of the New Halcyon model, seeded partly by returning diaspora from Country A's failed cities and partly by deliberate study of New Halcyon as a proof-of-concept. Its SEZ cities are younger and less stable than New Halcyon, still working through the same founding-era instabilities New Halcyon experienced in the 2040s–2060s.

Country F watches the Federal States / United Territories food dependency tension closely — its SEZ zones have their own food supply vulnerabilities and are studying New Halcyon's handling of the problem as a live case study.
