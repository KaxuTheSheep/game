# The United Territories

*Country G. The western and midland successor state. The Federal States' food supply and quiet leverage.*

---

## Overview

The United Territories formed when western and midland states refused to ratify [[The Catalyst (2039)|the 2039 bailout terms]] and broke away from what became [[The Federal States]]. It covers the bulk of the old country's landmass — West Coast, Midwest, Great Plains — and inherited both the agricultural heartland and the West Coast's technological and scientific base.

It is communist in structure: collectivised, state-managed, with genuine investment in public welfare. For the average citizen it is a better place to live than the Federal States by most measures — healthcare, nutrition, stability, quality of life. It is not a utopia; it has its own internal contradictions and pressures. But it has not collapsed, and in the absence of major external disruption, it is self-sustaining.

Its military is weak. This has never yet been fatally tested.

---

## Geography

West Coast, Midwest, Great Plains — the bulk of the old country's landmass. Includes former California (the scientific and technological inheritance), the Midwest breadbasket, and the agricultural river systems. The opposite of the Federal States: land-rich, resource-rich, population-distributed rather than coast-concentrated.

---

## Economy & Agriculture

The United Territories' farms are not farms in any traditional sense. They are GMO-based agricultural factories — industrial-scale bio-laboratories that happen to produce food. State-managed, scientifically intensive, oriented toward maximum nutritional yield rather than profit. More attuned to ecological sustainability than the Federal States' synthetic-food model, but not romanticised — these are factories, not gardens.

GMO food exports to the Federal States function, in practice, as micronutrient supplements for a population that would otherwise slowly degrade on synthetic calories alone. See [[GMO Agricultural Imports]] and [[Synthetic Food]].

---

## Technology

The West Coast inheritance gives the United Territories a genuine technological and scientific base — renewable energy systems, biotech, agricultural science, AI research oriented toward public welfare rather than profit extraction. It is technologically sophisticated, not primitive. What it is not is militarily oriented — its technology investment goes into life, not weapons.

---

## The Leverage Problem

The United Territories holds real power over the Federal States via food dependency, and knows it. A militarily weak nation that controls its stronger neighbour's food supply is in a precarious but defensible position — the deterrent is economic rather than military. This is currently holding. See [[Global Power Balance]] for how long that may last.
