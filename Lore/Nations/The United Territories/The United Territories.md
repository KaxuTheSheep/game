# The United Territories

*Country G. The western and midland successor state. The Federal States' food supply, biological component producer, and quiet leverage.*

---

## Overview

The United Territories formed when western and midland states refused to ratify [[The Catalyst (2039)|the 2039 bailout terms]] and broke away from what became [[The Federal States]]. It covers the bulk of the old country's landmass — West Coast, Midwest, Great Plains — and inherited both the agricultural heartland and the West Coast's technological and scientific tradition.

It is collectivist and state-managed, with genuine investment in public welfare, healthcare, and scientific research. For the average citizen it is a better place to live than the Federal States by most measurable standards. It is not a utopia. It has internal tensions, political rigidities, and its own forms of control. But it has not collapsed, and in the absence of major external pressure it is self-sustaining.

Its military is weak. This has not yet been fatally tested.

---

## Agriculture and Biotech

The United Territories' farms are not farms in any traditional sense. They are GMO-based agricultural factory-laboratories — industrial-scale biotech facilities producing food at high nutritional density under state management. More attuned to ecological sustainability than the Federal States' synthetic model, but not romanticised. These are facilities, not fields.

Their output — specialised GMO foods rich in micronutrients and biological compounds — is the mechanism of the Federal States' most significant strategic vulnerability. See [[GMO Agricultural Imports]] and [[FS-UT Relations]].

---

## Biological Components

The United Territories also produces the biological components — grown organs, non-Framework tissue, biotech replacements — required by Framework-augmented Federal States citizens who wish to de-augment or emigrate. This gives the UT a second lever of dependency over the Federal States beyond food.

Access to these components is controlled and dispensed selectively. The UT's policy is pragmatic rather than humanitarian — it assesses prospective recipients on usefulness and risk rather than need.

---

## The Managed Intake System

The UT does not operate an open-door emigration policy for Federal States citizens. From the UT perspective, an arriving FS citizen may be:

- Carrying corporate-linked or surveillance-capable hardware in their own body
- Culturally hostile after generations of FS propaganda
- Heavily dependent on SOMA infrastructure the UT does not broadly maintain
- Potentially valuable as a skilled worker
- Potentially a political or security liability

The UT therefore manages intake selectively and pragmatically. Those with skills it values receive more favourable assessment. Those without demonstrable value face a harder, slower process. This policy is not formally stated. It operates as institutional reality.

---

## The SOMA Question

The United Territories does not use the SOMA Framework in the way the Federal States does. Its enhancement model is biological — genetic engineering, biotech adaptation, integrated medical systems — rather than hardware and software layered onto the body.

The UT views SOMA-style augmentation as a dependency trap by design. The FS views the UT's biological model as paternalistic and innovation-limiting. Both assessments contain truth.

A Federal States citizen arriving in the UT is medically compatible with the UT's systems only after biological reconstruction — a process the UT controls, meaning arrival is not the same as integration.

---

## Technology

The West Coast inheritance gives the UT a genuine scientific and technological base — renewable energy, biotech, agricultural science, AI research oriented toward collective welfare. It is technologically sophisticated. What it is not is militarily oriented. Investment goes into life, not weapons.

---

## Leverage

The United Territories controls:
- The food supply that keeps the Federal States' population functional
- The biological components that Framework-augmented FS citizens need to leave

Both levers exist. Neither can be fully used without cost to the UT itself. This is the cold war equilibrium. See [[FS-UT Relations]].
