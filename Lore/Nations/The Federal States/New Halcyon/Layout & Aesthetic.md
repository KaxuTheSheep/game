# New Halcyon — Layout & Aesthetic

---

## Layout Philosophy

New Halcyon was never planned. Districts emerged from wherever conglomerate turf boundaries, leftover infrastructure, and faction control happened to settle — nothing ever smoothed it out. The result looks chaotic and feels chaotic, but the chaos has internal logic: follow the money, the territory, or the infrastructure and the shape of the city starts to make sense.

Quality of life is spatially legible. Road condition, lighting quality, building maintenance, and visible security presence all degrade the further you move from conglomerate cores toward forgotten districts. The city's inequality is literally mapped onto its geography.

## Structural Character

- High-tech slum — sprawling, dense, vertically chaotic
- No consistent zoning; residential, commercial, and industrial bleed into each other
- Road quality tracks corporate territory — maintained near conglomerate cores, deteriorating outward, ignored entirely in the furthest forgotten districts
- Visible faction presence is the primary "authority" signal — different uniforms, drone liveries, and colours mark whose turf you are in
- Old infrastructure (pre-collapse, pre-corporate) is visible underneath the new — repurposed, built over, occasionally still functional

## Districts

See [[Districts Overview]]. Districts are defined by culture, use, and territory rather than any formal zoning. Their boundaries are real but informal — marked by faction presence, building quality, and who you have to pay to feel safe.
