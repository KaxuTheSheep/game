# New Halcyon — Layout & Aesthetic

---

## Layout Philosophy

New Halcyon was never planned. Districts emerged from wherever conglomerate turf boundaries, leftover infrastructure, and faction control happened to settle — nothing ever smoothed it out. The result looks chaotic and feels chaotic, but the chaos has internal logic: follow the money, the territory, or the infrastructure and the shape of the city starts to make sense.

Quality of life is spatially legible. Road condition, lighting quality, building maintenance, and visible security presence all degrade the further you move from conglomerate cores toward forgotten districts. The city's inequality is literally mapped onto its geography.

For the full geographic picture — the estuary, the underground, the outskirts — see [[Geography Overview]].

---

## A City of Layers

New Halcyon expands in every direction simultaneously.

Above ground rise towers connected by enclosed skybridges, elevated transit, and suspended freight infrastructure. The web between the buildings defines the city more than any individual building does. Nothing exists in isolation — towers plug into larger systems rather than standing as independent monuments.

Below ground stretches another city. [[The Underground]] is not merely transportation infrastructure. It is inhabited — a self-organised urban environment with its own economy, culture, and social texture, built on the bones of an [[Ardent Systems]] project that was abandoned during [[The War]].

The surface is simply one layer within a much larger urban organism.

## Structural Character

- High-tech slum — sprawling, dense, vertically chaotic
- No consistent zoning; residential, commercial, and industrial bleed into each other
- Road quality tracks corporate territory — maintained near conglomerate cores, deteriorating outward, ignored entirely in the furthest forgotten districts
- Visible faction presence is the primary authority signal — different uniforms, drone liveries, and colours mark whose turf you are in
- Old infrastructure never disappears — it acquires new uses; buildings are repurposed rather than demolished; the city is archaeological rather than chronological
- Infrastructure is never hidden — service corridors, cooling systems, maintenance rails, drone access points are all expressed rather than concealed. See [[Infrastructure as Architecture]]

## The Outskirts

The edges of New Halcyon are marked by two distinct types of territory where the city's normal character gives way.

**The ruins pockets** — scattered fragments of pre-Catalyst New York construction at the outer margins, occupied by informal communities living in buildings that predate everything else about New Halcyon. No self-healing concrete, no building management systems, no compatible infrastructure. The visual contrast with the city proper is immediate and total.

**The Vantage ghost district** — a fully built but largely empty residential estate constructed during the founding period to non-standard specifications. The buildings heal themselves. Nobody lives in them. Vantage cannot reclaim it without methods disproportionate to what it is worth.

See [[The Outskirts]], [[The Ruins Pockets]], [[The Vantage Ghost District]].
