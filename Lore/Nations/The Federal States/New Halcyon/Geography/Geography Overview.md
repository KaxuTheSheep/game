# New Halcyon — Geography Overview

*A city built on the remains of another. Not planned. Accumulated.*

---

## The Geological Foundation

New Halcyon occupies the remains of New York City.

The AI failure during [[The Catalyst (2039)]] devastated much of the original city, permanently reshaping its coastline. Subsequent sea level rise flooded low-lying districts and widened the Hudson into a broad estuary. Parts of old Manhattan survive beneath newer construction. Others remain submerged or abandoned.

The city did not rebuild the old shoreline. It adapted to the new one.

---

## Primary Geographic Layers

New Halcyon is not a flat city. It exists across multiple vertical layers simultaneously.

**The Underground** — a self-organised urban environment beneath the surface, originally begun by [[Ardent Systems]] as transit and commercial infrastructure, abandoned during [[The War]], and subsequently claimed and developed by its current population. See [[The Underground]].

**The Surface** — the primary inhabited layer. Districts, towers, roads, faction territories. See [[Layout & Aesthetic]].

**The Estuary** — the Hudson, transformed by the [[Continental Freight Authority]] into the city's primary logistics spine. No longer a barrier. Part of the city. See [[The Estuary & CFA Spine]].

**The Outskirts** — the city's edges, where conglomerate control thins and disappears. Two distinct types. See [[The Outskirts]].

## Key Geographic Features

- [[The Estuary & CFA Spine]] — the CFA's artificial peninsula and port infrastructure; the city's economic backbone
- [[The Underground]] — inhabited sub-surface city; independent culture; Ardent-origin infrastructure
- [[The Outskirts]] — outer edges; two distinct zones
  - [[The Ruins Pockets]] — pre-Catalyst New York construction; occupied slums
  - [[The Vantage Ghost District]] — failed early Vantage housing estate; near-empty; self-healing

---

## What New Halcyon Is Not

New Halcyon was never planned. There is no unified geographic logic imposed from above. Districts emerged from ownership, conflict, and opportunity. Infrastructure accumulated rather than being replaced. The geography reflects decades of competing decisions made by entities with no obligation to coordinate.

The result is a city that is coherent without being uniform. Everything connects to everything else. Nothing was designed to.
