# The Estuary & CFA Spine

*The Hudson is no longer crossed. It is inhabited.*

---

## Overview

The Hudson River no longer resembles the waterway that defined old New York. Sea level rise following [[The Catalyst (2039)]] widened it into a broad estuary, flooding low-lying districts and permanently altering the coastline. Rather than fighting this geography, the [[Continental Freight Authority]] transformed it into the city's primary logistical asset.

The CFA spine is an immense artificial peninsula extending across the estuary. A primary structural spine crosses the water, from which modular dock districts, logistics platforms, and industrial branches extend outward. The structure is simultaneously port, freight terminal, industrial district, and foundation for future expansion.

The city did not expand onto the peninsula. The city attached itself to it.

---

## Vertical Layers

The estuary exists across multiple simultaneous layers rather than as a single surface.

**Above** — drone transit corridors, elevated freight movement, skybridges connecting the spine to surface districts, suspended logistics infrastructure.

**At water level** — active docks, ferry terminals, floating logistics platforms, CFA security perimeter, incoming freight processing.

**Below water** — utility tunnels, freight rail connections to [[The Underground]], maintenance access, data trunk routing. The vacuum optical trunk network passes through here connecting the CFA's port infrastructure to the city's backbone. See [[Network Infrastructure]].

---

## The Spine's Function

The spine is not a building. It is infrastructure at urban scale.

Its primary purpose is the movement and processing of goods entering New Halcyon. Everything the city imports — [[GMO Agricultural Imports]], biological components, manufactured goods, augment hardware — passes through CFA-controlled infrastructure here. This physical chokehold on the city's supply chains is the CFA's most concrete form of leverage, more tangible than any diplomatic relationship.

Secondary functions have accumulated over time: industrial processing facilities, warehousing, administrative infrastructure, and the security apparatus required to protect all of the above.

---

## The River as Connective Tissue

The estuary functions as connective tissue rather than a barrier. Several surface districts have grown along the waterfront specifically because of proximity to CFA logistics infrastructure — warehousing districts, freight-adjacent commercial zones, worker housing built by the CFA's own subsidiaries during the founding period.

Bridges crossing the estuary have acquired secondary uses over decades. Some carry transit. Others have accumulated commercial and residential occupation along their spans. The line between infrastructure and neighbourhood is not always clear.

---

## CFA Control

The spine and estuary are CFA territory. Their Tier 1 security apparatus operates here with no interference from other conglomerate forces — the only zone in New Halcyon where this is true. Every other conglomerate maintains working relations with the CFA and none have directly contested this control, partly because doing so would mean contesting an entity with [[Country B]]'s implicit backing, and partly because disrupting the import infrastructure would damage their own supply chains.

See [[Continental Freight Authority]] for the full picture of the CFA's position in New Halcyon and the internal fracture that may eventually change it.
