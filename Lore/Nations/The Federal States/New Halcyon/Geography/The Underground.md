# The Underground

*Ardent built it. The War emptied it. Everyone else filled it in.*

---

## Origin

The Underground began as an [[Ardent Systems]] infrastructure project during New Halcyon's founding period, predating [[The War]]. The original vision was transit-led commercial development — a subway network beneath the surface city, with commercial space integrated along the lines in the manner of an airport terminal. Transit as the spine. Retail, food, services as the body.

The AI systems installed to manage this infrastructure were primitive by later standards — early-generation automation appropriate to the 2040s, not the distributed multi-agent architecture of the present day. Some of this original infrastructure still functions in degraded form. Most has been repurposed beyond recognition.

Ardent withdrew incrementally as profits failed to materialise, as surface conflicts consumed capital, and eventually as [[The War]] made further underground investment impossible to justify. The withdrawal was not clean. It was abandonment — infrastructure left in place, access points unsealed, systems running on reduced maintenance until they adapted or failed.

The space did not stay empty.

---

## What It Became

The population that moved into the Underground did not arrive as a unified community with a shared plan. They arrived as individuals and small groups following opportunity — cheaper space, distance from surface faction control, proximity to transit infrastructure that still partially functioned.

What developed was not planned. It was evolutionary.

The Underground has its own economy, partly because the [[The Reserve|Reserve's]] credibility means even less at this remove from the surface's institutional infrastructure. Closed-community currencies and direct exchange dominate. See [[Currency & Alternatives]].

It has its own social texture — slang, conventions, rules of behaviour that emerged from the specific conditions of enclosed shared space rather than being imported wholesale from the surface. Surface faction structures have partial presence here but do not dominate. The Underground is not ungoverned. It governs itself through accumulated social contract rather than any formal structure.

Vertical movement between surface and Underground is common and unremarkable. The Underground is not a separate world. It is another layer of the same city, connected to the surface through entry districts whose culture reflects that connection. See [[Underground Entry Districts]].

## Levels

The Underground is organised across three functional levels. See [[The Underground Levels]] for full detail.

**Level -1** — the inhabited layer. Commercial streets, markets, restaurants, transit. The closest equivalent to a surface district. Quality varies but less extremely than on the surface.

**Level -2** — operational infrastructure. Loading docks, utilities, maintenance systems, robotic workers. Humans are present as inspectors and supervisors rather than permanent residents.

**Level -3** — restricted deep infrastructure. Water, power, data, waste systems. Authorised access only. The city's nervous system, effectively unseen by most residents.

---

## Relationship to Surface Factions

The Underground is not outside the city's faction structure. It is adjacent to it.

[[Commonweal]] has presence in parts of Level -1, consistent with their community-building orientation. Some gangs operate in the Underground's less organised sections. [[Greyback Company]] has no particular Underground footprint — their community embeddedness is surface-specific.

No single faction controls the Underground. The closest thing to dominant forces are the communities that have been there longest and accumulated the most social capital. These are not militias in the surface sense. They are simply established.

---

## Physical Character

The Underground retains significant visual evidence of its Ardent origin. Transit infrastructure — platforms, signage, circulation systems — survives in various states of repurposing. Original commercial units have been subdivided, extended, recombined. Ardent aesthetic language, the company's visual identity from the founding era, appears in fragments beneath decades of subsequent occupation.

The ceiling is low by surface standards. Lighting is artificial throughout. The absence of weather is both the Underground's primary comfort and its most alienating characteristic for new arrivals.
