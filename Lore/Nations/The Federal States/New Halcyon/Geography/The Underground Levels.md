# The Underground — Levels

*Three levels. One city. Most people only ever see the first one.*

---

## Level -1 — The Inhabited Layer

The layer most residents mean when they say "the Underground."

Originally designed by [[Ardent Systems]] as integrated transit and commercial infrastructure — the transit spine with retail, food and services built around it. The original vision is still legible in the layout. Platforms, circulation routes, and the remnants of Ardent's commercial unit grid all reflect the airport-terminal logic of the founding design.

What occupies those spaces now is entirely different.

**Commercial character:** Markets, food stalls, workshops, repair shops, service businesses. The economy here runs partly on [[Currency & Alternatives|closed-community currencies]] and direct exchange rather than Reserve currency. Surface franchise chains are present in the sections nearest to surface entry points. Further in, the commercial character becomes more independent and more specific to Underground culture.

**Transit:** Partial transit infrastructure still functions, maintained by a combination of community effort and residual automated systems. Routes are less predictable than surface transit. Knowledge of which lines are running on any given day is local knowledge rather than published information.

**Quality variation:** Level -1 is not uniformly anything. Sections near heavily used entry points are well-maintained and commercially active. Sections further from those anchors are quieter, cheaper, more independent. The variation is less extreme than surface district inequality — there is no Level -1 equivalent of a forgotten district — but it is present.

**Governance:** No single faction controls Level -1. Established communities, long-term residents, and informal social contracts provide structure. Surface factions have partial presence. [[Commonweal]] operates in several sections. Gangs operate in others. The majority of Level -1 is simply the Underground — governed by what has accumulated rather than what was imposed.

## Level -2 — Operational Infrastructure

The working layer. Not inhabited in the residential sense. Occupied by systems and the people who maintain them.

**Primary functions:** Loading docks receiving goods from surface freight routes and CFA-connected logistics channels. Utility distribution — the secondary layer where power, water and data from Level -3 are routed to surface and Level -1 destinations. Maintenance corridors. Workshop spaces for robotic system repair.

**Robotic presence:** Level -2 is the primary domain of automated maintenance and logistics systems. The robots operating here are purpose-built for their specific functions — logistics platforms, maintenance units, inspection systems. See [[Robotics]] and [[AI & Automation]]. The AI architecture managing Level -2 is more sophisticated than the original Ardent systems — upgraded incrementally over decades, never comprehensively replaced.

**Human presence:** Inspectors, supervisors, and specialist technicians descend to Level -2 when automated systems flag issues requiring human judgment. Some workers are based at Level -2 access points as permanent staff. Most humans treat Level -2 as a working environment rather than a place to inhabit.

**Access from Level -1:** Entry points from Level -1 to Level -2 are controlled but not uniformly secured. Some are formally restricted. Others are simply unmarked and therefore practically invisible to anyone without reason to look for them.

---

## Level -3 — Deep Infrastructure

The city's nervous system. Effectively invisible to almost everyone.

**What is here:** The primary water distribution network. Power infrastructure connecting to the surface grid and the CFA's energy systems. The vacuum optical data trunk network — the backbone of New Halcyon's communications infrastructure. See [[Network Infrastructure]]. Waste processing systems. The physical substrate of everything the surface city depends on.

**Access:** Formally restricted. Authorised personnel only, and the category of authorised personnel is narrow — infrastructure operators, emergency response teams, and the systems that manage the infrastructure autonomously. The average resident has no reason to come here and no mechanism to do so.

**The data question:** The vacuum optical trunks passing through Level -3 represent the most valuable physical target in New Halcyon's infrastructure. Data moving through the trunks is encrypted, but physical access to a trunk at a tap point provides the opportunity to install interception hardware. This has happened. The AI defensive systems managing Level -3 detect and dismantle interceptions when they find them. Some interceptions are found quickly. Others run for significant periods before discovery. The capability to successfully tap a Level -3 trunk and sustain it represents a serious technical and operational achievement — and the data retrieved would be correspondingly valuable. See [[Network Infrastructure]] and [[Pairing Protocol & Network Security]].

**Robotic defence:** Level -3 is actively patrolled by security-oriented automated systems in addition to maintenance robots. These are not the same category of system as Level -2 maintenance units. Their function is detection and response. Human security personnel are called for situations the automated systems cannot resolve.
