# New Halcyon — Founding Story

*Why this city survived when most of the others didn't.*

---

## The Site

New Halcyon occupies the remains of New York City.

The AI failure during [[The Catalyst (2039)]] devastated much of the original city, permanently reshaping its coastline. Subsequent sea level rise flooded low-lying districts and widened the Hudson into a broad estuary. Parts of old Manhattan survive beneath newer construction. Others remain submerged or abandoned in the pockets that would eventually become [[The Ruins Pockets]].

The site was chosen not despite this transformation but partly because of it. The broadened estuary created natural harbour conditions that no longer existed elsewhere on the eastern seaboard in the same configuration. Multiple major conglomerates converged on the same location for the same logistical reason.

---

## The Context

Following [[The Catalyst (2039)]], [[The Federal States]] produced Cities 1 through X — early attempts at fully privatised urban governance. The conditions were chaotic: no established template, competing corporate interests moving simultaneously, no state infrastructure to fall back on. Most cities either collapsed into ungoverned ruins, were absorbed piecemeal into conglomerate territories, or persist in degraded form.

New Halcyon is the exception.

## The CFA's Arrival

The [[Continental Freight Authority]] established itself in New Halcyon during the founding period — specifically during the later stages of [[The War]], when chaos in the city created gaps in logistics and port infrastructure that the CFA moved into with [[Country B]]'s backing.

The transformation of the estuary into the CFA spine was not planned as part of New Halcyon's founding vision. It emerged from the CFA's practical need for port infrastructure and their recognition that the broadened Hudson offered a natural foundation for large-scale logistics development. The spine's construction during and after the War period is one of the reasons the post-War city stabilised faster than it might otherwise have done — the CFA's infrastructure investment provided economic activity and supply chain function at a moment when the conglomerates were recovering from conflict.

See [[The Estuary & CFA Spine]] and [[Continental Freight Authority]].

## The Underground's Origin

[[Ardent Systems]], during the pre-War founding period, began constructing what would become [[The Underground]] — a transit and commercial infrastructure project beneath the surface city. The project was never completed. The War consumed the capital and attention that would have finished it.

The abandoned infrastructure was subsequently occupied and developed by the population that moved into it. What Ardent built as a commercial venture became something else entirely. See [[The Underground]].

---

## The War's Role

[[The War]] is inseparable from the founding story. New Halcyon came closest to joining the failed cities during the inter-conglomerate escalation conflict — the absence of any de-escalation mechanism meant a series of small disputes nearly destroyed the city before it had properly established itself. That it didn't is partly luck, partly the mutual recognition that total destruction served nobody's interests, and partly the exhaustion of all parties.

The settlement that ended the War is the direct origin of the present-day turf boundaries, faction arrangements, and grudges that structure the city.
