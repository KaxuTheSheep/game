# New Halcyon — Overview

*Official name: New Halcyon. Street names: New Hollow, The Halc. Usage varies by speaker and context — both are valid in-world.*

---

## What It Is

New Halcyon is a fully privatised megacity inside [[The Federal States]] — the surviving standout from Cities 1 through X, the Federal States' early experiments in corporate-administered urban governance. Most of those cities failed. New Halcyon did not. That survival, and what it cost, is the foundation of everything the city is now.

Multiple generations have grown up here with no living memory of a functioning government. Statelessness is not a wound in New Halcyon — it is the city's entire identity, its operating assumption, the water its inhabitants swim in.

---

## Character

New Halcyon feels like a high-tech slum — sprawling, dense, chaotic in layout, stratified in quality. The chaos is not authorial randomness; it is the direct product of a city that grew wherever conglomerate turf, faction control, and old infrastructure happened to settle, with no central planning ever smoothing the result. Districts emerged from use and territory rather than design.

Aesthetically: Cyberpunk 2077 scale and corporate-neon texture, Blade Runner rain-soaked noir and megabuilding oppression, Ghost in the Shell's AI-labour and body-modification undercurrent, Akira's urban density and institutional violence, Elysium's class stratification made spatial, Marathon's brutalist megastructures and retro-futurist runner/courier culture.

---

## Governance (or Lack of It)

None. No mayor, no council, no courts, no state police. Order — such as it is — is provided by:

- [[Tier 1 - Conglomerate Forces]] in corporate-owned territory
- [[Tier 2 - Protection Firms]] in residential areas, funded via rent
- [[Tier 3 - Militias & Gangs]] in forgotten and ungoverned districts

See [[Faction Overview]] for the full structure.
