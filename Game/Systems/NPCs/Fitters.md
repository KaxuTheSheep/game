# Fitters — Game Systems

*How fitters work as NPCs. For lore and profession detail see [[Lore/NPCs/Fitters]].*

---

## Finding Fitters

There is no universal first fitter. Players find them through:

- **Faction alignment** — conglomerate aligned starts give immediate access to tier 1 fitters through the conglomerate's own infrastructure
- **NPC guidance** — allies, contacts, and quest givers will direct players toward fitters appropriate to their current situation
- **Exploration** — fitters exist throughout New Halcyon, found by moving through districts and building local knowledge

Fitter quality and tier varies significantly by district and faction context. A forgotten district fitter and a conglomerate fitter are not equivalent options.

---

## Installation Quality

Every fitter NPC has an installation quality stat. This is fixed per NPC — it reflects their individual skill, not their tier, though tier and quality tend to correlate loosely.

Installation quality affects **bandwidth efficiency** of installed augments. A poor installation means an augment draws more bandwidth than its spec suggests. A high quality installation means the augment runs as efficiently as possible.

This matters most to players optimising tight bandwidth builds. Casual builds may not notice the difference. Heavily optimised builds feel it significantly.

Installation quality is not shown as a number to the player directly — it must be learned through reputation, NPC relationships, and word of mouth. Finding a high quality fitter in an unexpected location is genuinely valuable information.

---

## Reinstallation

Augments can be reinstalled by a different fitter to improve installation quality. This incurs a new installation fee but does not require purchasing the augment again.

A fitter who installs an augment they are still owed money on retains leverage over that augment until the debt is cleared.

---

## Payment Structure

Two separate costs exist for every augment installation:

**Augment cost**
The hardware itself. Paid in one of three ways:
- **Full purchase** — player owns the augment outright
- **Rental/subscription** — ongoing payment, augment remains functional while payments continue
- **Gifted** — provided by a faction or NPC, carries its own implications (see Debt below)

Fitters do not pay the full augment cost upfront themselves. They pay a down payment on installation and cover the remainder as the customer pays them. Higher tier fitters with corporate backing can front more expensive augments. Independent tier 2 fitters have real cash flow constraints on their stock.

**Installation cost**
The fitter's fee for the procedure itself. Paid mostly upfront. Instalment arrangements possible depending on the fitter relationship.

---

## Debt and Obligation

Augments carry different debt implications depending on how they were acquired:

**Rental/subscription debt**
Ongoing financial obligation to whoever holds the lease — manufacturer, conglomerate subsidiary, or the fitter themselves in some cases. Missing payments risks augment degradation or deactivation depending on contract terms. Often buried in the fine print of agreements players could read if they choose to.

**Surveillance debt**
Some gifted augments — particularly those from conglomerates — run corporate hardware that feeds data back to the provider. The augment works as advertised. The cost is information. This is not always disclosed explicitly.

**Loyalty debt**
Faction gifted augments may carry expectation of service. Manifests as quest obligations rather than explicit contract terms. Tracked through the faction relationship system.

---

## Killswitch

Some fitters retain remote access to augments they have installed while payment is outstanding. Use is rare — invoking it damages the fitter's community reputation, which independent tier 2 fitters depend on for referrals.

Most likely to occur on genuine non-payment rather than as a standard control mechanism.

Tier 1 conglomerate fitters may have killswitch capability built into standard contracts without it being prominently disclosed.

Tier 3 fitters generally lack the technical capability to implement this.

---

## Fitter Relationships

Fitters participate in the NPC relationship system fully. A fitter who trusts the player may:
- Front augments they would not normally risk on an unknown customer
- Source specific augments through their own supply network
- Warn the player if their hardware is being queried by outside parties
- Prioritise the player's reinstallation in time-sensitive situations

A fitter the player has defaulted on or damaged the relationship with may:
- Refuse service
- Sell the player's augment history to interested parties
- Flag outstanding hardware if they retain access
