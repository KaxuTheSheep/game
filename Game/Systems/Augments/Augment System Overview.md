# Augment System Overview

*How augmentation works as a game system. For lore context see [[Lore/Technology/Augments/Augment System Overview]].*

---

## Core Philosophy

Augments serve three functions simultaneously:
- **Build system** — what you can do in combat, dialogue, and exploration
- **Resource system** — what your framework can support and what you can afford
- **Narrative system** — what you owe and to whom

These are not separate modes. Every augment decision touches all three.

---

## The Dependency Chain

Augments are not independent items. They require other augments or the framework itself to function. The chain runs:

**Framework → Core Stack → Everything Else**

Nothing outside the core stack functions without the relevant core stack component being installed. See [[Core Stack]] for prerequisites.

---

## Port Logic

Ports are communication channels in the framework. Any augment that sends or receives data uses a port. Purely structural augments do not.

**Uses ports:**
- All core stack components communicating with each other
- Eyes, audio, input sensors — transmitting sensory data
- Arms, legs — sending and receiving movement and feedback data
- Full body dermal augments — receiving activation signals
- Organ augments that actively monitor or regulate biological systems

**Does not use ports:**
- Skeleton — purely structural
- Subdermal sectional armour — passive
- Joints — mechanical and structural only

Port count is determined by framework tier and model. Exceeding available ports means an augment cannot be installed regardless of bandwidth availability.

---

## Two Costs

Every augment installation has two separate costs:

1. **Augment cost** — the hardware itself, paid in full or via ongoing rental/subscription
2. **Installation cost** — the fitter's fee, mostly paid upfront, installments possible

These are paid to different parties and carry different implications. See [[Game/Systems/NPCs/Fitters]] for the full economic model.

---

## Augment Tiers and Models

Within any augment type, two axes of choice exist:

- **Model** — the manufacturer's design philosophy. Different models prioritise different stats within the same augment type. Horizontal choice.
- **Tier** — quality within a model. Tiers 1 through 5, vertical quality ladder. Higher tier is strictly better within the same model.

Cross model and tier comparison is a build question. A Tier 2 of one model may suit a specific build better than a Tier 4 of another.

---

## Player Entry Point

Players start with a base SOMA framework and no augments beyond the core stack. Augment access from there depends on:

- Faction alignment determining fitter access
- Reputation determining what missions are available
- Finances determining what can be afforded or rented

There is no fixed first fitter. Players find fitters through NPC guidance or exploration.
