# Framework Tiers

*The base layer that determines everything else. Choose your ceiling carefully.*

---

## SOMA Framework Overview

SOMA frameworks vary across four stats:

- **Bandwidth ceiling** — maximum load before overload states trigger
- **Port count** — hard limit on connected augments, linked to bandwidth
- **Battery capacity** — how long before power becomes critical
- **Recharge rate** — speed of both wireless sleep recharge and wired fast charge

Higher ports naturally come with higher bandwidth to support them. A framework optimised for ports is therefore also increasing bandwidth capacity as a consequence.

---

## Models

Models represent design philosophy within the SOMA standard. All tiers exist within each model. Choosing a model is choosing what your framework prioritises.

**Model X — Bandwidth Focused**
Maximises processing ceiling. Fewer ports than Y at equivalent tier but higher bandwidth per port. Suits builds running fewer, more demanding augments.

**Model Y — Port Focused**
Maximises connectivity. More ports than X at equivalent tier, bandwidth scales to match. Suits builds running many augments simultaneously at lower individual draw.

**Model Z — Balanced**
Neither extreme. A tier 5 Z exceeds a tier 2 X or Y in both stats, but a tier 5 X or Y will outperform tier 5 Z in their respective specialisation. Suits players who want flexibility without committing to a philosophy.

Model choice is not about which is objectively better. It is about which suits the build you are running.

---

## Tiers

All tiers exist within all models. Tiers are a vertical quality ladder within the same model — higher tier is strictly better than lower tier of the same model.

**Tier 1**
Minimal bandwidth ceiling. Enough for core stack and a small number of basic augments. Low port count. Short battery life requiring active power management. Slow recharge on both methods. Entry level — what most people receive with basic conglomerate employment packages or buy as their first framework.

**Tier 2**
Moderate bandwidth. Core stack plus a meaningful augment selection without significant constraint. Adequate battery for normal daily use. Standard recharge rates. The workhorse tier — most of New Halcyon's augmented population sits here permanently.

**Tier 3**
Comfortable bandwidth ceiling. Builds begin to have real identity and specialisation. Port count stops being a limiting factor for most builds. Good battery life — power only becomes critical under sustained heavy use. Faster recharge on both methods. Independent professionals, successful militia members, mid tier conglomerate employees.

**Tier 4**
High bandwidth. Approaching what most players need for advanced or heavily specialised builds. Ports effectively unrestricted for practical purposes. Strong battery — power management largely a non issue under normal conditions. Fast recharge.

**Tier 5**
Effectively uncapped for any realistic build. No practical port restrictions. Maximum battery capacity and fastest recharge on both methods. Rare, expensive, accessed through high level conglomerate relationships or black market channels.

## Proprietary Frameworks

Proprietary frameworks sit entirely outside the tier and model structure. Each is a fixed, complete package — no variants, no upgrades within it. You either want that specific framework or you do not.

Proprietary frameworks are named unique items tied to specific conglomerates. Each has its own characteristics defined by its maker rather than following the SOMA stat model.

General proprietary characteristics:
- Resistance to external scanning and hacking — the primary advantage
- Locked augment compatibility — only approved hardware functions, a significant cost given the SOMA augment catalogue
- Corporate monitoring built in — a liability for most players, potentially acceptable for fully conglomerate aligned builds
- Integrated architecture — NI and MPU potentially merged, different physical layout to SOMA

Proprietary frameworks are viable only for players whose build and faction alignment make the tradeoffs worthwhile. For most players SOMA is the correct choice.

Named proprietary frameworks are documented under their respective conglomerate files.
